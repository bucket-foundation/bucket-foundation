# 10 — Earth Sciences

> **What counts as canon-tier in earth sciences.** A foundational identification of a planetary process (deep time, plate tectonics, glacial cycles, the carbon cycle), a stratigraphic principle that organizes the geological record, a quantitative model of an earth system, or an observational dataset that forces such a model. Refining a measurement is not canon-tier; the figures below each forced or enabled a structural reframing of how the planet is understood.

### Nicolas Steno (Niels Stensen)

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | steno |
| **lifespan** | 1638–1686 |
| **era** | Scientific Revolution |
| **region / tradition** | Copenhagen, Florence — Danish anatomy and natural history |
| **primary works** | *De solido intra solidum naturaliter contento dissertationis prodromus* (1669, Latin) |

**Foundation contribution.** Established the **founding principles of stratigraphy**: (1) the *law of superposition* — in an undisturbed sequence of sedimentary layers, the lower layers were deposited before the upper; (2) the *principle of original horizontality* — sedimentary layers are originally deposited horizontally; (3) the *principle of lateral continuity* — layers extend laterally until they thin out or meet a barrier. Recognized that fossils are the remains of once-living organisms (against the older view that they were *lusus naturae*, jokes of nature).

**Why canon.** Stratigraphy is the substrate of geological reasoning. Without these three principles, the rock record cannot be read; with them, the entire history of the Earth becomes a recoverable sequence.

**Downstream.** Hutton, Smith (the geological map), Lyell, all of historical geology.

**Tags.** `stratigraphy` `superposition` `original-horizontality` `fossils-as-organisms`

### James Hutton

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | hutton |
| **lifespan** | 1726–1797 |
| **era** | Scottish Enlightenment |
| **region / tradition** | Edinburgh — Scottish geology |
| **primary works** | *Theory of the Earth* (paper read 1785; expanded into two volumes, 1795, English) |

**Foundation contribution.** Founded **deep time**. By examining the unconformity at Siccar Point — a discordance between two rock sequences that themselves were the products of long deposition, lithification, tilting, erosion, and renewed deposition — Hutton concluded that the Earth must be vastly older than the few thousand years biblical chronology allowed. His famous formulation: the Earth shows *"no vestige of a beginning, no prospect of an end."*

**Why canon.** Without deep time, evolution by natural selection is impossible (there isn't enough time for it to operate); plate tectonics is impossible; the rest of geology is impossible. Hutton is the figure who first *saw* the necessary timescales by reading them out of the rocks themselves.

**Downstream.** Lyell, Darwin, all of historical geology and evolutionary biology.

**Tags.** `deep-time` `uniformitarianism` `unconformity` `siccar-point`

### Charles Lyell

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | lyell |
| **lifespan** | 1797–1875 |
| **era** | Early to mid-19th century |
| **region / tradition** | London — British geology |
| **primary works** | *Principles of Geology* (3 vols, 1830–1833, English) |

**Foundation contribution.** Systematized and popularized **uniformitarianism** — the methodological principle that the geological processes operating today (erosion, sedimentation, volcanism, faulting) are the same processes that operated in the past, and that the present is therefore *the key to the past*. The *Principles* gave the discipline a coherent framework and a textbook synthesis that made Hutton's deep time the working assumption of geology.

**Why canon.** Methodology, not new data. Uniformitarianism becomes the operating procedure of historical geology, and the methodological move it represents — *no special past causes are needed to explain the past* — is the same kind of move Newton made for celestial mechanics and Darwin would make for biology.

**Downstream.** Darwin (who carried *Principles* on the *Beagle* voyage); all of 19th-century historical geology.

**Tags.** `uniformitarianism` `principles-of-geology` `methodological-naturalism` `historical-geology`

### Alfred Wegener

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | wegener |
| **lifespan** | 1880–1930 |
| **era** | Early 20th century |
| **region / tradition** | Marburg, Hamburg, Graz — German meteorology and geophysics |
| **primary works** | *Die Entstehung der Kontinente und Ozeane* (1915, German) |

**Foundation contribution.** Proposed **continental drift**: the continents were once joined in a single supercontinent (*Pangaea*) and have since drifted apart, and the apparent fit of the African and South American coastlines, the matching fossil distributions across now-separated continents, and the geological similarities of distant mountain ranges are evidence of this. He had no convincing mechanism, and the theory was rejected by the geological mainstream for nearly fifty years.

**Why canon.** The empirical case — the continents fit together and the fossil record knows it — was correct. The mechanism (plate tectonics driven by mantle convection, with seafloor spreading at mid-ocean ridges) was supplied between 1962 and 1968 by Hess, Vine, Matthews, and Wilson, vindicating Wegener decades after his death on a Greenland ice cap.

**Downstream.** Hess (seafloor spreading, 1962); Vine and Matthews (magnetic stripes, 1963); the plate tectonics revolution of the late 1960s; all of modern geophysics.

**Tags.** `continental-drift` `pangaea` `delayed-canon` `posthumous-vindication`

### Milutin Milanković

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | milankovic |
| **lifespan** | 1879–1958 |
| **era** | Early to mid-20th century |
| **region / tradition** | Belgrade — Serbian astronomy and geophysics |
| **primary works** | *Kanon der Erdbestrahlung und seine Anwendung auf das Eiszeitenproblem* (1941, German) |

**Foundation contribution.** Calculated the **astronomical forcing of Earth's climate**: variations in the Earth's orbital eccentricity (~100,000-year cycle), axial obliquity (~41,000 years), and axial precession (~26,000 years) modulate the seasonal and latitudinal distribution of solar insolation, and these variations are sufficient to drive the timing of glacial and interglacial periods. The work was done largely by hand from a Hungarian internment camp during World War I.

**Why canon.** Provides the first quantitative astronomical theory of Quaternary climate change. Confirmed by deep-sea sediment records and ice-core data in the 1970s (Hays, Imbrie, Shackleton, "Pacemaker of the Ice Ages," 1976), at which point the Milanković cycles became canonical.

**Downstream.** All of paleoclimatology; the modern interpretation of ice-core and ocean-sediment records; the climate-forcing framework that anthropogenic climate change is now understood against.

**Tags.** `milankovic-cycles` `orbital-forcing` `paleoclimate` `ice-ages`

### Marie Tharp

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | tharp |
| **lifespan** | 1920–2006 |
| **era** | Mid-20th century |
| **region / tradition** | Lamont Geological Observatory, Columbia — American marine geology |
| **primary works** | World Ocean Floor Panorama (with Bruce Heezen, 1977); the Atlantic seafloor maps (1957 onward) |

**Foundation contribution.** Mapped the **Mid-Atlantic Ridge and its central rift valley** from sounding data, and recognized that the rift was a continuous global feature running through every ocean basin. Her maps provided the visual evidence for **seafloor spreading** that helped settle the continental drift debate in favor of plate tectonics. She was initially told by her male collaborator that her interpretation must be wrong because it implied continental drift — which it did, and which was correct.

**Why canon.** The visual evidence that ended the continental drift debate. Once the ridge system was mapped and recognized as a connected planetary feature, Wegener's framework became unavoidable.

**Downstream.** The plate tectonics revolution; all subsequent ocean-floor mapping; the modern view of the Earth as a tectonically active planet.

**Tags.** `mid-atlantic-ridge` `seafloor-mapping` `rift-valley` `plate-tectonics-evidence`
