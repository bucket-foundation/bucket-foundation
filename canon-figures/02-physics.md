# 02 — Physics

> **What counts as canon-tier in physics.** A new conservation law, a new symmetry principle, a new equation of motion that survives experimental contact, a new state of matter or substrate (field, quantum, spacetime) that reorganizes the discipline, or an experimental result that forces such reorganization. Refinement of a parameter, no matter how precise, is not canon-tier. The figures below each forced a structural change.

---

### Ibn al-Haytham (Alhazen)

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | ibn-al-haytham |
| **lifespan** | c.965–c.1040 |
| **era** | Islamic Golden Age |
| **region / tradition** | Cairo (Fatimid) — Arabic optics & natural philosophy |
| **branches** | 02-physics |
| **cross-branches** | 01-mathematics, 07-mind |
| **primary works** | *Kitāb al-Manāẓir* (*Book of Optics*, c.1011–1021, Arabic) |

**Foundation contribution.** Founded **optics as an experimental science**. Showed that vision is the result of light *entering* the eye, not light *leaving* it (overturning the Greek extramission theory). Designed and described the camera obscura. Developed an early version of the experimental method: hypothesis → controlled experiment → mathematical analysis → conclusion.

**Why canon.** Two foundations in one work: the correct theory of vision and the methodological commitment to experimentation as the arbiter of physical claims. Both are foundational, not technical.

**Downstream.** Kepler's *Astronomiae Pars Optica* (1604), Roger Bacon, Witelo, the entire optical tradition that runs to Newton's *Opticks*.

**Tags.** `optics` `vision` `experimental-method` `camera-obscura`

### Galileo Galilei

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | galileo |
| **lifespan** | 1564–1642 |
| **era** | Late Renaissance |
| **region / tradition** | Padua, Florence — Italian natural philosophy |
| **branches** | 02-physics |
| **cross-branches** | 06-cosmology |
| **primary works** | *Sidereus Nuncius* (1610, Latin); *Dialogo sopra i due massimi sistemi del mondo* (1632, Italian); *Discorsi e dimostrazioni matematiche intorno a due nuove scienze* (1638, Italian) |

**Foundation contribution.** Three foundations: (1) **the law of free fall** ($s \propto t^2$), the first quantitative law of mechanics; (2) **the principle of relativity** — physics inside a uniformly moving ship is indistinguishable from physics at rest, the seed of every later relativity principle; (3) **telescopic confirmation of heliocentrism** — Jupiter's moons, Venus's phases, lunar craters — turning Copernicus's hypothesis into observational fact.

**Why canon.** The shift from "philosophy of nature" to "physics as a mathematical-experimental science" happens here. The *Discorsi* is the first textbook of mechanics in the modern sense.

**Downstream.** Newton, Huygens, every later inertial physics.

**Tags.** `free-fall` `relativity-principle` `telescope` `mechanics`

### Isaac Newton

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | newton |
| **lifespan** | 1642–1727 |
| **era** | Scientific Revolution |
| **region / tradition** | Cambridge — English natural philosophy |
| **branches** | 02-physics |
| **cross-branches** | 01-mathematics, 06-cosmology |
| **primary works** | *Philosophiæ Naturalis Principia Mathematica* (1687, Latin); *Opticks* (1704, English); *De Methodis Serierum et Fluxionum* (c.1671, Latin) |

**Foundation contribution.** The **three laws of motion** + the **law of universal gravitation** unified terrestrial and celestial mechanics under a single mathematical framework, establishing that the same equations describe an apple falling and the Moon orbiting the Earth. Co-developed the **calculus** as the mathematical language required to express those laws. The *Principia* is the founding text of mathematical physics.

**Why canon.** This is the first complete mathematical theory of nature. Every subsequent physical theory is either an extension or a replacement of it.

**Downstream.** Lagrange, Hamilton, Laplace, Maxwell, Einstein (who replaced it where it failed).

**Disputed.** The calculus priority dispute with Leibniz is real but irrelevant to canon status; both arrived at the foundation independently.

**Tags.** `mechanics` `gravitation` `calculus` `unification`

### Émilie du Châtelet

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | du-chatelet |
| **lifespan** | 1706–1749 |
| **era** | French Enlightenment |
| **region / tradition** | Paris, Cirey — French natural philosophy |
| **branches** | 02-physics |
| **cross-branches** | 01-mathematics |
| **primary works** | *Institutions de Physique* (1740, French); translation of and commentary on Newton's *Principia* (completed 1749, published 1759) |

**Foundation contribution.** Established **kinetic energy as $mv^2$, not $mv$**, by analyzing experiments on lead balls dropped into clay. This settled the *vis viva* dispute that had divided Cartesians (who held $mv$) from Leibnizians (who held $mv^2$) for forty years and is the conceptual ancestor of $\tfrac{1}{2}mv^2$ and energy conservation. Her annotated French translation of the *Principia* remains the standard French edition.

**Why canon.** Energy as a conserved physical quantity is foundational; the $v^2$ scaling is the right answer; she is the figure who got it experimentally and explained it. The *vis viva* debate is one of the few foundational disputes that was resolved by a single decisive experimental analysis.

**Downstream.** Lagrange, Helmholtz, Joule, conservation of energy as a foundational principle.

**Tags.** `kinetic-energy` `vis-viva` `principia-translation` `energy-conservation`

### James Clerk Maxwell

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | maxwell |
| **lifespan** | 1831–1879 |
| **era** | Victorian Britain |
| **region / tradition** | Edinburgh, Cambridge — Scottish/English mathematical physics |
| **branches** | 02-physics |
| **primary works** | *On Physical Lines of Force* (1861, English); *A Dynamical Theory of the Electromagnetic Field* (1865, English); *A Treatise on Electricity and Magnetism* (1873, English) |

**Foundation contribution.** Wrote down the **four equations** that unify electricity, magnetism, and optics. The crucial step is the introduction of the **displacement current** term, which lets the equations predict electromagnetic waves traveling at the speed of light — and which therefore *identifies* light as an electromagnetic phenomenon. Light is not *like* electromagnetism; it *is* electromagnetism.

**Why canon.** First field-theoretic unification in physics. Sets the template for every later unification (electroweak, GUT, etc.). The displacement-current move is one of the great theoretical inferences of the 19th century.

**Downstream.** Hertz (experimental confirmation, 1887), Lorentz, Einstein (whose 1905 SR paper explicitly cites Maxwell), QED.

**Tags.** `electromagnetism` `field-theory` `displacement-current` `unification`

### Ludwig Boltzmann

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | boltzmann |
| **lifespan** | 1844–1906 |
| **era** | Late 19th century |
| **region / tradition** | Vienna, Graz, Munich, Leipzig — Austrian/German physics |
| **branches** | 02-physics |
| **cross-branches** | 04-information |
| **primary works** | *Weitere Studien über das Wärmegleichgewicht unter Gasmolekülen* (1872, German); *Über die Beziehung zwischen dem zweiten Hauptsatze...* (1877, German) |

**Foundation contribution.** Founded **statistical mechanics** by deriving thermodynamic quantities from the microscopic statistics of molecules. The equation $S = k_B \log W$ (engraved on his tombstone) defines entropy in terms of the *number of microstates compatible with a given macrostate* — the bridge between probability and thermodynamics.

**Why canon.** Reduces thermodynamics to mechanics + statistics. The information-theoretic reading of entropy ($S = -k_B \sum p_i \log p_i$) makes Boltzmann a direct ancestor of Shannon (1948) and of every subsequent statistical-mechanics-meets-information-theory result.

**Downstream.** Gibbs, Einstein (1905 Brownian motion paper, which finally convinced atomists), Shannon, the entire field of statistical mechanics and statistical inference.

**Tags.** `statistical-mechanics` `entropy` `microstates` `kinetic-theory`

### Albert Einstein

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | einstein |
| **lifespan** | 1879–1955 |
| **era** | 20th century |
| **region / tradition** | Bern, Berlin, Princeton — German/Swiss/American physics |
| **branches** | 02-physics |
| **cross-branches** | 06-cosmology |
| **primary works** | The four 1905 Annus Mirabilis papers (German); *Die Grundlage der allgemeinen Relativitätstheorie* (1916, German) |

**Foundation contribution.** Three foundations in one year (1905), one more in 1915: (1) **special relativity** — the constancy of the speed of light forces space and time into a unified spacetime; (2) **the photoelectric effect** — light is quantized, founding (with Planck) the photon concept; (3) **Brownian motion** — the statistical fluctuations of pollen grains prove atoms exist; (4) **general relativity** (1915) — gravity is the curvature of spacetime, replacing Newton's action-at-a-distance with a geometric field equation.

**Why canon.** Each of the four is sufficient by itself for canon status. GR is the working theory of gravity, cosmology, and large-scale spacetime. SR is the ground floor of every relativistic theory. The photon paper opened quantum mechanics. The Brownian paper closed the atomism debate.

**Downstream.** All of 20th-century physics.

**Tags.** `special-relativity` `general-relativity` `photon` `brownian-motion`

### Emmy Noether

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | noether |
| **lifespan** | 1882–1935 |
| **era** | Early 20th century |
| **region / tradition** | Göttingen, then Bryn Mawr — German mathematical physics |
| **branches** | 02-physics |
| **cross-branches** | 01-mathematics |
| **primary works** | *Invariante Variationsprobleme* (1918, German) |

**Foundation contribution.** Proved **Noether's theorem**: every continuous symmetry of a physical system corresponds to a conserved quantity. Time-translation symmetry → conservation of energy. Spatial-translation symmetry → conservation of momentum. Rotational symmetry → conservation of angular momentum. Gauge symmetries → conserved currents. Conservation laws are not separate facts; they are *consequences of symmetries*.

**Why canon.** Reframes all of physics around symmetry. The Standard Model of particle physics is *built* on Noether's theorem — every gauge boson corresponds to a gauge symmetry, and every gauge symmetry yields a conserved charge. Without Noether's theorem the modern particle physics architecture is unintelligible.

**Downstream.** Yang–Mills, the Standard Model, every gauge theory, every conservation-law derivation in modern physics.

**Tags.** `symmetry` `conservation-laws` `gauge-theory` `noether-theorem`

### Niels Bohr

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | bohr |
| **lifespan** | 1885–1962 |
| **era** | Early 20th century |
| **region / tradition** | Copenhagen — Danish physics, Copenhagen interpretation |
| **branches** | 02-physics |
| **primary works** | *On the Constitution of Atoms and Molecules* (1913, English); the Como lecture (1927) on complementarity |

**Foundation contribution.** Proposed the **quantized atom**: electrons orbit the nucleus only at discrete allowed energy levels and emit/absorb photons when they jump between them. The 1913 paper explained the hydrogen spectrum from first principles, fixing the failure of classical electromagnetism (which predicts the atom is unstable) by *postulating* quantization. Later, formulated **complementarity** — the principle that wave and particle descriptions of quantum entities are mutually exclusive but jointly exhaustive.

**Why canon.** The 1913 paper is the founding document of modern atomic theory. Complementarity is the conceptual anchor of the Copenhagen interpretation, which remained the dominant interpretation of quantum mechanics for the rest of the 20th century.

**Downstream.** Heisenberg, Pauli, Born, the entire Copenhagen school.

**Tags.** `quantum-atom` `complementarity` `quantization` `copenhagen-interpretation`

### Paul Dirac

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | dirac |
| **lifespan** | 1902–1984 |
| **era** | 20th century |
| **region / tradition** | Cambridge, Princeton — English theoretical physics |
| **branches** | 02-physics |
| **primary works** | *The Quantum Theory of the Electron* (1928, English); *The Principles of Quantum Mechanics* (1930, English) |

**Foundation contribution.** Wrote down the **Dirac equation**, the relativistic wave equation for the electron that combines special relativity with quantum mechanics. The equation predicted the existence of **antimatter** as a mathematical consequence — an entire new class of particles inferred from a missing minus sign. Discovered four years later (Anderson, 1932). Also gave the first formulation of quantum field theory and the bra–ket notation that became standard.

**Why canon.** First successful unification of SR and QM. The prediction of antimatter is one of the cleanest cases in physics history of mathematics dictating the existence of something nobody had imagined.

**Downstream.** Feynman, Schwinger, Tomonaga (QED), the entire Standard Model.

**Tags.** `dirac-equation` `antimatter` `relativistic-quantum` `qft-foundations`
