# Polyglot Register

> **A derived view over `figures.json`, plus landscape-only entries that do not rise to canon-tier but are load-bearing for the polyglot phenomenon itself.**
>
> **Editorial bar.** "Knew many languages" is not the test. The test is: **was multilingualism the epistemic instrument that produced the canonical contribution?** If removing the multilingualism removes the work, the figure qualifies. If the languages were merely biographical, they don't.
>
> Champollion and Ventris are obvious yes: both worked by cross-language phonetic identification, and neither's contribution exists without polyglot practice. William Jones the same — the Indo-European hypothesis *is* the simultaneous comparison of Sanskrit, Greek, Latin, Gothic, Celtic, and Persian. Tolkien is yes with a different shape: the creative work is constructed on philological method, not decorated by it.
>
> Mezzofanti is the borderline: a fluent speaker of reportedly 30+ languages who produced no original scholarly work. Included as a **landscape** figure because the polyglot phenomenon is itself historically interesting, with an explicit caveat.
>
> Generated 2026-04-23 (pass 2).

## Landscape-only polyglots (not canon-tier, listed for completeness)

These figures are **not** added to `figures.json` as canon cards. They are listed here because the polyglot phenomenon is historically load-bearing and the register is incomplete without them.

| Figure | Lifespan | What | Why landscape, not canon |
|---|---|---|---|
| Giuseppe Mezzofanti | 1774–1849 | Cardinal and Vatican library prefect, reportedly fluent in 30+ languages (documented by Charles W. Russell, 1858) | No original scholarly corpus. The polyglot phenomenon is the entire record; that is historically interesting but not a foundation-tier contribution in the canon sense. |
| Heinrich Schliemann | 1822–1890 | Businessman turned excavator of Troy (Hissarlik), claimed mastery of ~15 languages acquired by aggressive self-taught method | Excavation methods were destructive and chronology often wrong; the polyglot autobiography is partly promotional. Canon of archaeology at Troy belongs to Dörpfeld and later excavators. Included as landscape because the *linguistic-preparation-as-research-discipline* model he popularized is historically real. |
| Richard Francis Burton | 1821–1890 | British explorer, translator of *1001 Nights* and *Kama Sutra*, reported 25+ languages | Genuine linguistic competence and significant translation output, but the ethnographic claims are colonial-era and the original-contribution threshold for canon-tier is not cleanly met. Landscape figure for the polyglot-as-comparative-ethnographer pattern. |
| Kató Lomb | 1909–2003 | Hungarian interpreter; *Polyglot: How I Learn Languages* (1970) is a foundational personal methodology for adult language acquisition | Not a linguist in the research sense; a practitioner whose methodological memoir is a real document of polyglot practice. Landscape for the practitioner-methodology side of the phenomenon. |

## What this register is not

- Not a "languages spoken" list. Language count is not the bar; **epistemic load-bearing** is.
- Not fixed. If a future pass promotes Mezzofanti or Burton to canon-tier on different grounds, they move out of "landscape" and into the top table with a full card.
