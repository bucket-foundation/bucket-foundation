# 05 — Biophysics

> **What counts as canon-tier in biophysics.** A foundational identification (DNA, the gene, the action potential, the Krebs cycle), a structural determination that explains a function (alpha helix, double helix, hemoglobin), a mechanism that becomes the substrate of further work (chemiosmosis, voltage-gated ion channels), or a substrate-level theory of life (germ theory, central dogma, neuron doctrine). The figures below each added one of these.

---

### Gregor Mendel

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | mendel |
| **lifespan** | 1822–1884 |
| **era** | Mid-19th century |
| **region / tradition** | Brno (Augustinian abbey) — Moravian/Czech experimental natural history |
| **primary works** | *Versuche über Pflanzen-Hybriden* (1866, German) |

**Foundation contribution.** Demonstrated that **inheritance is particulate**, not blending. By tracking quantitative ratios of traits (round vs. wrinkled peas) over multiple generations, he inferred the existence of discrete heritable factors (later called *genes*), each in two copies per organism, that segregate independently and recombine randomly in the offspring.

**Why canon.** The single foundational claim of genetics. The 1866 paper sat unread for 34 years (rediscovered 1900 by de Vries, Correns, Tschermak) because nobody had the conceptual category for "discrete factor" yet — exactly the kind of foundation a canon should preserve regardless of its century of reception.

**Downstream.** All of classical and molecular genetics, population genetics, the modern synthesis.

**Disputed.** R. A. Fisher's 1936 statistical re-analysis suggested the published ratios are *too good* to be experimentally honest. Most historians now accept that Mendel reported the *correct* underlying biology, with possible publication-cleanup.

**Tags.** `inheritance` `gene` `segregation` `independent-assortment`

### Louis Pasteur

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | pasteur |
| **lifespan** | 1822–1895 |
| **era** | Mid-19th century |
| **region / tradition** | Paris, Strasbourg, Lille — French chemistry & microbiology |
| **branches** | 05-biophysics |
| **cross-branches** | 03-chemistry |
| **primary works** | *Mémoire sur la fermentation appelée lactique* (1857, French); experimental refutation of spontaneous generation (1859–61) |

**Foundation contribution.** Established the **germ theory of disease** by showing that fermentation and putrefaction are caused by living microorganisms, not by abiotic chemistry. The swan-neck flask experiments definitively refuted spontaneous generation. Developed vaccination as a generalizable method (rabies, anthrax) on the basis of attenuated pathogens.

**Why canon.** Reorients medicine from humoral and miasmatic theories to a microbial-causation framework. Every subsequent advance in infectious-disease medicine and microbiology presupposes this shift.

**Downstream.** Koch, Lister, the entire field of microbiology, modern vaccinology.

**Tags.** `germ-theory` `fermentation` `vaccination` `spontaneous-generation`

### Rosalind Franklin

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | franklin |
| **lifespan** | 1920–1958 |
| **era** | Mid-20th century |
| **region / tradition** | King's College London, Birkbeck — English X-ray crystallography |
| **primary works** | *Molecular Configuration in Sodium Thymonucleate* (1953, English, *Nature*); the X-ray diffraction images of B-form DNA (1952) |

**Foundation contribution.** Produced the X-ray diffraction images of B-form DNA — most famously **"Photograph 51"** — that contained the geometric evidence for the helical structure of DNA: ten residues per turn, 34 Å pitch, double-helical symmetry. Her measurements of the unit cell dimensions and her identification of the A and B forms were the structural data on which the Watson–Crick model was built.

**Why canon.** The double helix is one of the most consequential structural identifications in the history of science. Franklin produced the experimental evidence that constrained the model. Without Photograph 51 the helical model is speculation.

**Disputed.** Photograph 51 was shown to Watson by Wilkins without Franklin's knowledge. Watson, Crick, and Wilkins shared the 1962 Nobel; Franklin had died of ovarian cancer in 1958 (Nobel rules at the time excluded posthumous awards). The historical record on attribution is the subject of an ongoing literature (most recently Cobb & Comfort 2023).

**Tags.** `dna-structure` `x-ray-crystallography` `photograph-51` `b-form-dna`

### James Watson & Francis Crick

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | watson-crick |
| **lifespan** | Watson 1928– ; Crick 1916–2004 |
| **era** | Mid-20th century |
| **region / tradition** | Cambridge (Cavendish, MRC LMB) — Anglo-American molecular biology |
| **primary works** | *Molecular Structure of Nucleic Acids: A Structure for Deoxyribose Nucleic Acid* (1953, English, *Nature*) |

**Foundation contribution.** Proposed the **double-helix model of DNA** with Watson–Crick base pairing (A–T, G–C). The model immediately suggested its own copying mechanism: each strand serves as a template for the other. The famous closing line — *"It has not escaped our notice that the specific pairing we have postulated immediately suggests a possible copying mechanism for the genetic material"* — is one of the largest understatements in the history of science.

**Why canon.** Identifies the molecular substrate of inheritance. Connects Mendel's discrete factors to a physical structure with explicit base-pairing rules. Crick later (1958, 1970) formulated the **central dogma** of molecular biology — sequence information flows DNA → RNA → protein, with no reverse path from protein to nucleic acid sequence.

**Downstream.** All of molecular biology; the genetic code; Sanger sequencing; the Human Genome Project.

**Tags.** `double-helix` `base-pairing` `central-dogma` `genetic-material`

### Alan Hodgkin & Andrew Huxley

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | hodgkin-huxley |
| **lifespan** | Hodgkin 1914–1998; Huxley 1917–2012 |
| **era** | Mid-20th century |
| **region / tradition** | Cambridge, Plymouth — English neurophysiology |
| **primary works** | The five-paper series in *J. Physiol.* (1952, English), culminating in *A Quantitative Description of Membrane Current and its Application to Conduction and Excitation in Nerve* |

**Foundation contribution.** Wrote down the **Hodgkin–Huxley equations** — a quantitative biophysical model of the action potential in the squid giant axon, in which voltage-gated sodium and potassium conductances generate the spike. The model is derived from voltage-clamp recordings, fits the experimental data, and *predicts* the propagating action potential and conduction velocity from first principles.

**Why canon.** First successful first-principles model of a *biological* dynamical system. The action potential is the universal currency of nervous-system computation; without the Hodgkin–Huxley model, computational neuroscience has no founding equation.

**Downstream.** All of computational neuroscience; ion-channel biology; the entire literature on neural excitability.

**Tags.** `action-potential` `voltage-clamp` `ion-channel` `hodgkin-huxley-equations`

### Peter Mitchell

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | mitchell |
| **lifespan** | 1920–1992 |
| **era** | Mid-20th century |
| **region / tradition** | Edinburgh, Glynn Research Institute — English biochemistry |
| **primary works** | *Coupling of Phosphorylation to Electron and Hydrogen Transfer by a Chemi-Osmotic Type of Mechanism* (1961, English, *Nature*) |

**Foundation contribution.** Proposed the **chemiosmotic hypothesis**: ATP synthesis in mitochondria and chloroplasts is driven by an electrochemical proton gradient across a membrane, not by a high-energy chemical intermediate. The membrane-embedded electron transport chain pumps protons across the membrane; the resulting gradient powers ATP synthase, which acts as a molecular turbine.

**Why canon.** Replaces the dominant chemical-intermediate paradigm with a structural, membrane-based mechanism. Mitchell was outside the mainstream and was rejected for over a decade before the experimental case became overwhelming. Awarded the 1978 Nobel in Chemistry alone — one of the rare unshared chemistry Nobels.

**Downstream.** All of bioenergetics, the entire study of mitochondrial function, the mechanistic understanding of ATP synthase as a rotary motor.

**Tags.** `chemiosmosis` `proton-gradient` `atp-synthesis` `bioenergetics`

### Lynn Margulis

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | margulis |
| **lifespan** | 1938–2011 |
| **era** | Late 20th century |
| **region / tradition** | Boston University, UMass Amherst — American evolutionary biology |
| **primary works** | *On the Origin of Mitosing Cells* (1967, English, *J. Theor. Biol.*); *Symbiosis in Cell Evolution* (1981, English) |

**Foundation contribution.** Proposed the **endosymbiotic theory of eukaryotic origins**: mitochondria and chloroplasts are descendants of free-living bacteria that were engulfed by a host cell and retained as obligate endosymbionts. Predicted independent prokaryote-like genomes inside both organelles — confirmed by molecular evidence in the following two decades.

**Why canon.** Restructures the tree of life: the eukaryotic cell is not the result of internal differentiation alone but of a *merger* between distinct ancestral lineages. Margulis was rejected by 15 journals before the 1967 paper was finally published. The theory is now the textbook account.

**Downstream.** All of cell evolution; the three-domain tree of life; the modern understanding of horizontal gene transfer; the LUCA debate.

**Tags.** `endosymbiosis` `mitochondria` `chloroplast` `eukaryogenesis`

### Albert Szent-Györgyi

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | szent-gyorgyi |
| **lifespan** | 1893–1986 |
| **era** | 20th century |
| **region / tradition** | Szeged, Woods Hole — Hungarian/American biochemistry |
| **branches** | 05-biophysics |
| **cross-branches** | 03-chemistry |
| **primary works** | Isolation of hexuronic acid / vitamin C (1928–32, English); *Bioenergetics* (1957, English); *Introduction to a Submolecular Biology* (1960, English) |

**Foundation contribution.** Isolated **vitamin C** from adrenal cortex and paprika, and identified the four-carbon dicarboxylic acids (fumarate, malate, succinate, oxaloacetate) that Krebs later assembled into the citric-acid cycle. In the later part of his career proposed that **electronic energy states of proteins** — not only chemistry at the level of substrate-and-enzyme — carry biological information, opening the electronic/quantum biology question later taken up by Ling, Pollack, and others.

**Why canon.** The vitamin C and Krebs-precursor work is uncontroversially foundation-tier. The submolecular-biology programme is contested but historically generative: it names the problem that biophysics has still not closed.

**Downstream.** Krebs (citric-acid cycle); Gilbert Ling; modern redox biology; the quantum-biology research programme.

**Tags.** `vitamin-c` `citric-acid-cycle` `bioenergetics` `submolecular-biology`

### Harold Saxton Burr

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | burr |
| **lifespan** | 1889–1973 |
| **era** | Early to mid-20th century |
| **region / tradition** | Yale School of Medicine — American neuroanatomy and bioelectrics |
| **branches** | 05-biophysics |
| **cross-branches** | |
| **primary works** | *The Electro-Dynamic Theory of Life* (with F. S. C. Northrop, *Quarterly Review of Biology*, 1935, English); *Blueprint for Immortality* (1972, English) |

**Foundation contribution.** Measured **steady DC voltage gradients** ("L-fields," life-fields) across whole organisms — trees, salamanders, humans — using high-impedance voltmeters, and showed that these fields vary with developmental stage, injury, ovulation, and sleep. Proposed that the organism's morphology is maintained by a standing electrical template.

**Why canon.** First systematic experimental programme asserting that **bioelectric fields carry morphogenetic information**, a claim now re-entering the mainstream through Levin and others. Burr's specific mechanistic claims are disputed; the empirical phenomenon (measurable whole-organism DC fields correlated with physiological state) is not.

**Downstream.** Becker, Marino, Levin; modern bioelectric-medicine research.

**Disputed.** Burr's "field = blueprint" interpretation is not accepted; the measurements themselves have been replicated.

**Tags.** `bioelectric-field` `l-field` `morphogenesis` `dc-gradient`

### Robert O. Becker

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | becker |
| **lifespan** | 1923–2008 |
| **era** | Mid-20th century |
| **region / tradition** | Syracuse VA Hospital, SUNY Upstate — American orthopedic surgery and bioelectrics |
| **branches** | 05-biophysics |
| **cross-branches** | |
| **primary works** | *The bioelectric factors in amphibian-limb regeneration* (*Journal of Bone and Joint Surgery*, 1961, English); *The Body Electric* (1985, English) |

**Foundation contribution.** Demonstrated that **limb regeneration in salamanders is driven by endogenous DC currents** produced by dedifferentiating cells at the wound site, and showed that imposed currents of the right polarity could induce partial regeneration in adult frogs (which normally cannot regenerate). Established the bioelectric signal as a causal, not merely correlational, factor in tissue patterning.

**Why canon.** Moves bioelectric phenomena from descriptive measurement (Burr) to causal mechanism: current polarity and magnitude control regeneration. The substrate for modern electroceutical and regenerative-medicine work.

**Downstream.** Andrew Marino (Becker's collaborator); Michael Levin; the field of bioelectric medicine.

**Tags.** `regeneration` `dc-current` `dedifferentiation` `bioelectric-medicine`

### Andrew Marino

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | marino |
| **lifespan** | 1939– |
| **era** | Late 20th / early 21st century |
| **region / tradition** | LSU Health Shreveport — American bioelectrics and biophysics |
| **branches** | 05-biophysics |
| **cross-branches** | |
| **primary works** | *Electromagnetism & Life* (with R. O. Becker, 1982, English); *Modern Bioelectricity* (ed., 1988, English); papers on non-linear dynamics of EEG response to weak electromagnetic fields |

**Foundation contribution.** Extended Becker's bioelectric programme into the study of **biological response to weak electromagnetic fields**, and demonstrated — using non-linear dynamical analysis of EEG — that nervous systems respond deterministically to fields below the then-assumed thermal-noise threshold. Established detection methodology distinguishing genuine field-coupling from artifact.

**Why canon.** (Living-figure card; single contribution.) Puts the low-field-bioeffects question on formal non-linear-dynamics footing rather than statistical-epidemiology footing. Whether the effects are clinically significant remains debated; the detection framework is the foundation.

**Downstream.** Low-intensity EMF bioeffects literature; bioelectric-medicine measurement protocols.

**Tags.** `emf-bioeffects` `non-linear-eeg` `low-field-detection` `living-figure`

### Gilbert Ling

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | ling |
| **lifespan** | 1919–2019 |
| **era** | Mid-20th to early 21st century |
| **region / tradition** | Pennsylvania Hospital, Damadian Foundation — American/Chinese cell physiology |
| **branches** | 05-biophysics |
| **cross-branches** | 03-chemistry |
| **primary works** | *A Physical Theory of the Living State* (1962, English); *Life at the Cell and Below-Cell Level* (2001, English) |

**Foundation contribution.** Proposed the **association-induction (AI) hypothesis**: cellular potassium/sodium asymmetry is not maintained by membrane pumps but by **adsorption of K⁺ onto fixed anionic sites on cytoplasmic proteins**, with cell water structured in multiple layers around those proteins. Predicted NMR-detectable differences between bulk and intracellular water — confirmed experimentally by Damadian and others, and the basis on which Damadian invented MRI.

**Why canon.** A rival foundation to the membrane-pump paradigm that dominates cell biology. Ling's specific claims about the Na/K-ATPase are not accepted; his claims about structured cell water, and the experimental programme that led to MRI, are historically decisive.

**Downstream.** Damadian (MRI); Gerald Pollack; the structured-water research programme.

**Disputed.** AI hypothesis as a full replacement for membrane transport is rejected by mainstream cell biology. The intracellular-water-structure observations are not.

**Tags.** `association-induction` `structured-water` `cell-potassium` `mri-precursor`

### Gerald Pollack

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | pollack |
| **lifespan** | 1939– |
| **era** | Late 20th / early 21st century |
| **region / tradition** | University of Washington — American bioengineering |
| **branches** | 05-biophysics |
| **cross-branches** | 03-chemistry |
| **primary works** | *Cells, Gels and the Engines of Life* (2001, English); *The Fourth Phase of Water* (2013, English) |

**Foundation contribution.** (Living-figure card; single contribution.) Identified and characterized the **exclusion zone (EZ) of water** adjacent to hydrophilic surfaces — a macroscopic (hundreds of micrometers) layer that excludes solutes, carries a net negative charge, and has spectroscopic properties distinct from bulk water. Built on Ling's structured-water prediction; the EZ phenomenon has been replicated across multiple labs and surface types.

**Why canon.** If the EZ is as broadly biologically relevant as Pollack argues, it reframes cell water from a passive solvent into an active structural/energetic component. Mainstream biophysics accepts the phenomenon and disputes its scope; the phenomenon itself is the foundation contribution.

**Downstream.** The structured-water and interfacial-water research programmes.

**Disputed.** The biological scope of EZ water is disputed; the physical existence of the EZ is not.

**Tags.** `exclusion-zone` `ez-water` `interfacial-water` `fourth-phase` `living-figure`

### Fritz-Albert Popp

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | popp |
| **lifespan** | 1938–2018 |
| **era** | Late 20th century |
| **region / tradition** | Marburg, Kaiserslautern, IIB Neuss — German biophysics |
| **branches** | 05-biophysics |
| **cross-branches** | |
| **primary works** | *Biophoton Emission* (Popp, Li, Gu, eds., 1992, English); *Electromagnetic Bio-Information* (ed., 1979, English) |

**Foundation contribution.** Detected and characterized **ultra-weak photon emission from living tissue** (biophotons) using photomultiplier counting, and showed that the emission statistics are non-thermal (closer to a coherent source than to blackbody radiation). Established biophoton measurement as a reproducible experimental technique.

**Why canon.** Whether biophotons carry biological information, as Popp argued, remains contested; that living tissue emits detectable, non-thermally-distributed photons is now an accepted experimental phenomenon. The measurement programme is the foundation.

**Downstream.** Biophoton research groups in Germany, Japan, China, Russia; ultra-weak-photon-emission oxidative-stress assays.

**Disputed.** Popp's coherence and information-storage interpretations are not mainstream; the emission itself is.

**Tags.** `biophoton` `ultra-weak-emission` `photon-counting` `non-thermal`

### Michael Levin

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | levin |
| **lifespan** | 1969– |
| **era** | Early 21st century |
| **region / tradition** | Tufts University Allen Discovery Center — American developmental bioelectrics |
| **branches** | 05-biophysics |
| **cross-branches** | 07-mind |
| **primary works** | *Bioelectric mechanisms in regeneration* (*Annual Review of Biomedical Engineering*, 2007, English); *The Computational Boundary of a "Self"* (*Frontiers in Psychology*, 2019, English) |

**Foundation contribution.** (Living-figure card; single contribution.) Demonstrated that **voltage-gradient patterns across non-neural tissue carry instructive morphogenetic information** — that altering the pre-pattern of transmembrane voltage in planaria and Xenopus produces predictable anatomical changes (two-headed planaria, ectopic eyes). Formalized the non-neural bioelectric code as a real causal layer between genotype and morphology.

**Why canon.** Vindicates the Burr/Becker bioelectric programme with rigorous molecular tools and reproducible outcomes. Reframes the genotype-phenotype map to include a bioelectric intermediate layer.

**Downstream.** Bioelectric medicine; synthetic biology of anatomy (xenobots); basal cognition research.

**Tags.** `bioelectric-code` `morphogenesis` `planaria` `xenobots` `living-figure`

### Nick Lane

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | lane |
| **lifespan** | 1967– |
| **era** | Early 21st century |
| **region / tradition** | University College London — English evolutionary biochemistry |
| **branches** | 05-biophysics |
| **cross-branches** | |
| **primary works** | *The Vital Question* (2015, English); *Transformer* (2022, English); papers on alkaline-hydrothermal-vent origin-of-life (with W. Martin, 2012–present) |

**Foundation contribution.** (Living-figure card; single contribution.) Articulated the **natural proton-gradient origin of life at alkaline hydrothermal vents**: that geochemically-generated pH gradients across thin inorganic membranes at submarine alkaline vents provide the ancestral proton-motive force later internalized by chemiosmotic cells, solving the long-standing paradox of where the energy currency of life came from before cells existed.

**Why canon.** Takes Mitchell's chemiosmotic principle and extends it backward from LUCA to the prebiotic geochemistry that produced LUCA — a mechanistically specific, geologically constrained origin-of-life hypothesis.

**Downstream.** Alkaline-vent origin-of-life research programme; bioenergetic reframings of eukaryogenesis.

**Tags.** `origin-of-life` `alkaline-vents` `proton-gradient` `chemiosmosis-extended` `living-figure`

### Arturo Solís-Herrera

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | solis-herrera |
| **lifespan** | 1950– |
| **era** | Early 21st century |
| **region / tradition** | Aguascalientes — Mexican medicine (ophthalmology / biophysics) |
| **branches** | 05-biophysics |
| **cross-branches** | 03-chemistry |
| **primary works** | *The Human Photosynthesis* (2011, English); papers on melanin-mediated water dissociation (2002–present, English/Spanish) |

**Foundation contribution.** (Living-figure card; single contribution.) Reported that **melanin catalyzes the dissociation of water into molecular hydrogen and oxygen in the presence of visible light**, proposing an endogenous "human photosynthesis" pathway. The specific efficiency numbers and systemic-physiology claims are disputed; the underlying electrochemistry (melanin as a broadband absorber with redox activity on water) is a measurable phenomenon.

**Why canon.** Either the effect is significant and melanin is an energy-transducing pigment in animals, or it is not and the claim joins the catalog of instructive biophysical near-misses. Included in the index so that the question is not lost.

**Disputed.** The quantitative and clinical claims are not accepted by mainstream physiology. The core electrochemistry is replicable.

### Vladimir Khavinson

| | |
|---|---|
| **id** | khavinson |
| **lifespan** | 1946– |
| **era** | Late 20th / early 21st century |
| **region / tradition** | St Petersburg Institute of Bioregulation and Gerontology — Russian gerontology |
| **branches** | 05-biophysics |
| **cross-branches** | |
| **primary works** | *Peptides and Ageing* (2002, English); clinical trials on short peptide bioregulators (1980s–present, Russian/English) |

**Foundation contribution.** (Living-figure card; single contribution.) Characterized a family of **short (di-, tri-, tetra-peptide) tissue-specific bioregulators** isolated from organ extracts, and reported in long-running Soviet/Russian clinical trials that oral administration of these peptides modulates organ-specific gene expression and lifespan endpoints in elderly cohorts. **Why canon.** Represents a coherent non-Western biogerontology research programme with decades of data; included so that the programme is indexed rather than invisible.

**Disputed.** Mechanism and effect sizes are not accepted by mainstream Western gerontology; the peptide-isolation chemistry is not disputed.
