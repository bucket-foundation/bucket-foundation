# art-pedagogy/

**Tier: landscape (pedagogical lineages + creative-practice popularizers + creativity research). Not canon — but contains peer-reviewed creativity-research candidates flagged for promotion to `bucket-canon/07-mind/creativity/`.**

How people are taught to make art, how people are taught to be "creative," and what (if anything) the research literature says about either. Bucket separates three tiers inside this folder:

1. **Craft pedagogy** (atelier, Bauhaus, Beaux-Arts) — technique traditions with long in-use track records. Landscape.
2. **Creative-practice popularizers** (Cameron, Kleon, Rubin, etc.) — self-help-tradition books that move public behavior. Landscape, not canon.
3. **Creativity research** (Guilford, Amabile, Csikszentmihalyi, Simonton, Eisner) — peer-reviewed. Promotion candidates.

## 1. Historical pedagogical lineages

- **French Académie Royale / Beaux-Arts** — 17th-19th century; cast-drawing → life-drawing → composition ladder. Still the DNA of most Western academic art education.
- **Bauhaus** (Weimar 1919 → Dessau 1925 → Berlin 1932 → closed 1933) — Itten/Albers/Moholy-Nagy preliminary course (*Vorkurs*); unified fine-art + craft + industrial design.
- **Black Mountain College** (1933–1957, North Carolina) — Albers, Cage, Rauschenberg, de Kooning, Merce Cunningham. Experimental interdisciplinary.
- **Cooper Union** (NY; free tuition tradition until 2014) — rigorous drawing foundation.
- **RISD** (Providence) — craft + fine-art parity.
- **Yale MFA** — postwar American painting / sculpture pipeline (Albers → Guston → Tuttle → many).
- **CalArts** (Valencia, CA) — Disney-founded 1961; Conceptualist turn under John Baldessari et al.
- **Goldsmiths** (London) — YBA pipeline (Hirst, Emin, etc., late 1980s onward under Michael Craig-Martin).
- **Art Students League of New York** (atelier tradition, 1875-).
- **Russian academy** lineage — Repin Academy (St Petersburg) → Stroganov (Moscow) → Surikov (Moscow); late-Soviet and post-Soviet preservation of 19th-century academic method.
- **Florence atelier revival** — Angel Academy of Art (Florence), Florence Academy of Art, Barcelona Academy of Art, Grand Central Atelier (NYC). Deliberate revival of pre-modernist sight-size method.

## 2. Classical drawing primaries (craft canon — long-standing, often in print)

- Bargue, *Cours de dessin*, 1868–71 — **public domain**
- Bridgman, *Constructive Anatomy*, 1920 — **public domain (US)**
- Nicolaides, *The Natural Way to Draw*, 1941
- Loomis, *Figure Drawing for All It's Worth*, 1943 — **license flag: US copyright was restored post-2000; not PD despite long out-of-print gap.** Estate reissues via Titan Books.
- Loomis, *Creative Illustration*, 1947 — same license status
- Hogarth, *Dynamic Anatomy*, 1958
- Hale, *Drawing Lessons from the Great Masters*, 1964
- Vilppu, *The Vilppu Drawing Manual* (self-published, multiple editions)

These are tools-of-the-trade, not axioms. They stay landscape; they don't promote to canon even if they're useful, because they encode method rather than derive it.

## 3. Creative-practice popularizers (landscape, explicitly not canon)

- Cameron, *The Artist's Way*, 1992 — morning pages + artist dates. Self-help tradition.
- Pressfield, *The War of Art*, 2002 — "Resistance" as antagonist.
- Tharp, *The Creative Habit*, 2003 — choreographer's practice notebook.
- Kleon, *Steal Like an Artist*, 2012; *Show Your Work*, 2014.
- Lamott, *Bird by Bird*, 1994 — writing-adjacent; commonly cited in art-practice contexts.
- Rubin, *The Creative Act: A Way of Being*, 2023.

Format note: these are memoir + aphorism + exercise. They move behavior (measurable by sales and citation), but they don't establish claims. Landscape by construction.

## 4. Art-education research (peer-reviewed; borderline canon candidates)

- Eisner, *The Educational Imagination*, 1979
- Eisner, *The Arts and the Creation of Mind*, 2002
- Robinson, *Out of Our Minds*, 2001 + Ken Robinson's 2006 TED talk (TED is not canon; the underlying research is)

Flag: peer-reviewed, but arguments are largely normative ("schools should teach art"). Promotion depends on whether the specific claim being promoted is a derived empirical finding or a policy recommendation.

## 5. Creativity research — PROMOTION CANDIDATES to `bucket-canon/07-mind/creativity/`

These are peer-reviewed primary research and are the serious intellectual substrate of the field:

- **Guilford 1950**, *American Psychologist* 5(9):444, "Creativity" — the divergent-thinking founding paper.
- **Torrance Tests of Creative Thinking** (TTCT) — Torrance 1966 onward; psychometric instrument with longitudinal data.
- **Amabile 1983**, *The Social Psychology of Creativity*; **1996** *Creativity in Context* — componential theory (domain skills × creativity skills × intrinsic motivation).
- **Csikszentmihalyi 1990**, *Flow: The Psychology of Optimal Experience*; **1996**, *Creativity: Flow and the Psychology of Discovery and Invention*.
- **Simonton 1988**, *Scientific Genius*; **1999**, *Origins of Genius: Darwinian Perspectives on Creativity*; plus extensive primary papers in *Psychological Review*, *Psychological Bulletin*.

**Recommendation**: open `bucket-canon/_intake/creativity/` with Guilford 1950, Amabile 1983, Csikszentmihalyi 1990, Simonton 1999 as the first four; promote individual claims only when method + replication are confirmed.

## 6. The how-to-be-artistic gap

Most popular "how to be creative" content recycles Cameron + Kleon + misattributed Picasso quotes. Readers looking for substance should read, in order:

- **Craft**: Nicolaides, Bargue, Bridgman, Loomis
- **Research**: Guilford, Amabile, Csikszentmihalyi, Simonton, Eisner

Bucket indexes both tiers, but labels them clearly. The self-help tradition is not invalid; it is just a different kind of object from peer-reviewed creativity research.
