# research-landscape/

**Sibling to `bucket-canon/`. Not canon.**

This directory holds material that Bucket Foundation tracks but does not treat as foundation-tier:

- Hypothesis-tier claims that have not passed peer-reviewed replication
- Popular synthesis, commentary, and podcasts that move public understanding
- Marketplace / commercial / regulatory landscape data (situational awareness)
- Philosophy-tier shelves (distinct from science canon)
- Comparative / interpretive work that indexes patterns rather than deriving them from axioms

## Why this exists

Canon holds only foundations. But a lot of interesting, useful, and directionally correct material does not meet the canon bar. Rather than either (a) ignore it or (b) contaminate canon, Bucket keeps a parallel landscape directory. Landscape material can be promoted to canon when a peer-reviewed primary source confirms a specific claim; canon material can be demoted to landscape if retracted.

## What lives here

```
research-landscape/
  history-commentariat/          — Hancock, Carlson, podcasters, etc.
  deep-history-hypotheses/       — alt-dating, catastrophism syntheses (to be created)
  myth-patterns/                 — comparative mythology, Hamlet's Mill etc.
  biophysics/
    becker-commentariat/         — interviews, talks, commentary about Becker
  philosophy/
    longtermism/                 — Bostrom, Ord, MacAskill etc.
  peptide-markets/               — RUO vendor landscape (read-only, non-facilitation)
```

## Promotion / demotion rules

- **Landscape → Canon**: requires a peer-reviewed primary source with replicable method supporting the specific claim being promoted. The commentator who first surfaced the claim does not automatically get canon status; the primary paper does.
- **Canon → Landscape**: happens on retraction or serious methodological refutation. The demoted work is preserved (not deleted) and the retraction itself is cited.

## Editorial constraints

- No hagiography.
- No "recovered suppressed truth" framing.
- Landscape is honest about its own status — every file should say what tier it is and why.
- Commercial content (peptide-markets, etc.) is read-only and non-facilitating. Bucket indexes information; it does not enable transactions.
