# myth-patterns/

Comparative mythology pattern-hunting. **Landscape tier.** The patterns are real; the causal claims drawn from them are hypothesis-tier.

## Why this is landscape, not canon

Canon requires primary-source derivation. A cross-cultural flood motif is a real pattern, but "therefore there was a global flood" or "therefore ancient peoples encoded astronomical knowledge in myth" are hypotheses that require evidence beyond the pattern itself. The pattern lives here; primary archaeoastronomy papers (if any) live in `bucket-canon/08-deep-history/archaeoastronomy/`.

## Motif catalog (seed)

### Flood myths
- Sumerian: Ziusudra / Atra-Hasis
- Akkadian: Utnapishtim (Gilgamesh Tablet XI)
- Hebrew: Noah (Genesis 6–9)
- Greek: Deucalion and Pyrrha
- Hindu: Manu and Matsya
- Mesoamerican: various (Popol Vuh 1st creation cycle; Aztec Tezcatlipoca)
- Aboriginal Australian: Tiddalik variant and regional flood stories
- Native American: multiple (Hopi, Inuit, Nootka)
- Chinese: Gun-Yu flood
- Lithuanian: Pramžimas

**Scholarship**: Dundes 1988 *The Flood Myth* (comparative anthology); Frazer 1918 *Folk-lore in the Old Testament* (Victorian, landscape-tier use only); Witzel 2012 *The Origins of the World's Mythologies* (Harvard, the most rigorous modern comparative treatment).

### Precession-encoded myth (Hamlet's Mill thesis)
- de Santillana & von Dechend 1969 *Hamlet's Mill* — the foundational landscape work
- Thesis: ancient mythic systems encode precession of the equinoxes as a "mill that grinds" / axial tilt story
- **Status**: hypothesis-tier. Critiqued in academic archaeoastronomy (Ruggles, Aveni) for selection bias. Interesting but not promotable without specific primary evidence per claim.

### Sky-change / celestial-disturbance motifs
- Phaethon (Greek)
- "When the sun stood still" / Joshua 10:13
- Chinese "ten suns" (Archer Yi)
- Ragnarök solar darkening
- Possible correlations with historical eclipses, comets, and (speculatively) Miyake events — the Miyake events themselves are canon in `08-deep-history/cataclysms/`; *correlation with myth* is landscape.

### Fire from the sky
- Sodom and Gomorrah
- Phaethon again
- Quetzalcoatl / Tollan fire
- **Relevance**: some YDIH proponents (Moore 2020 Abu Hureyra) link Genesis-19-type destructions to impact airbursts. Primary paper is canon; the mythic correlation is landscape.

### Dragon / serpent / chaos motifs
- Tiamat, Leviathan, Jörmungandr, Typhon, Vritra, the Rainbow Serpent
- Often associated with waters / chaos / pre-creation
- Scholarship: Gunkel 1895 *Schöpfung und Chaos* (Chaoskampf); Forsyth 1987 *The Old Enemy*

### Lost-continent motifs
- Atlantis (Plato: *Timaeus*, *Critias*) — primary texts are canon; "Atlantis was real" is landscape-tier hypothesis
- Mu / Lemuria — 19th-century fabrications, well-documented as such; included as landscape only to flag them as NOT real
- Thule, Hyperborea — Greco-Roman literary constructs

## Key scholars (landscape tier — most are canon-figures candidates)

- **Georges Dumézil** — trifunctional Indo-European hypothesis; influential, partly critiqued
- **Mircea Eliade** — sacred/profane, eternal return; 20th-century comparativist
- **Joseph Campbell** — monomyth; pop-scholarly, useful entry-point, primary-weak
- **Giorgio de Santillana & Hertha von Dechend** — *Hamlet's Mill*
- **E.C. Krupp** — modern skeptical archaeoastronomy (canon-adjacent)
- **Anthony Aveni** — Mesoamerican archaeoastronomy (canon)
- **Clive Ruggles** — British archaeoastronomy (canon)
- **Michael Witzel** — Harvard comparative mythology (canon)

## Editorial stance

Patterns are real. Causal claims drawn from patterns require independent evidence. This folder indexes the patterns honestly and notes where specific claims have been promoted to canon (or demoted from it).
