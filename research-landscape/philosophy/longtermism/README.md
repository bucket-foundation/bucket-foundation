# philosophy/longtermism/

**Philosophy tier, landscape.** Longtermism is a moral philosophy, not a science. It does not enter Bucket's science canon (which holds only foundations / axioms / primary derivations) but it is tracked here because longtermist frameworks inform how Bucket Foundation thinks about research prioritization.

## Core texts

### Foundational
- **Derek Parfit** — *Reasons and Persons* (1984), Part IV. The identity + future-generations arguments that make longtermism technically possible.
- **Nick Bostrom** — *Superintelligence* (2014); *Deep Utopia* (2024); "Existential Risk Prevention as Global Priority" (2013, *Global Policy* 4:15).
- **Toby Ord** — *The Precipice* (2020). Best single entry-point; rigorous risk estimates + policy framing.
- **William MacAskill** — *What We Owe the Future* (2022); *Doing Good Better* (2015).

### Adjacent
- **Peter Singer** — *The Life You Can Save* (2009); utilitarian base layer.
- **Hilary Greaves** — "Population axiology" (Philosophy Compass 2017); technical foundations for comparing future lives.
- **Amanda Askell** — "Pareto principles in infinite ethics" (2018). Edge-cases.
- **Stuart Armstrong, Anders Sandberg** — existential-risk technical papers from FHI.

### Critics (balance)
- **Émile P. Torres** — *Human Extinction: A History of the Science and Ethics of Annihilation* (2023); prolific longtermism critic.
- **Phil Torres / Timnit Gebru / EleutherAI critics** — "TESCREAL" framing
- **Amia Srinivasan, Olúfẹ́mi Táíwò** — procedural-justice critiques of effective-altruism-adjacent longtermism

## Why Bucket tracks this

1. **Bucket's mission is long-horizon**: "paid for once, citeable forever." Longtermist discounting + future-generation arguments give the *philosophical* ground for why primary research should be preserved indefinitely.
2. **Canon-selection has a longtermist shape**: we index foundations *because* foundations compound over centuries; outcome-tier work does not.
3. **Risk literature is real**: The Precipice's risk estimates (natural pandemics, engineered pandemics, AI, nuclear, unknown) inform which canon branches deserve effort.

## What Bucket rejects about longtermism

- **Fanaticism about astronomical-future values** (Bostrom "astronomical waste" arguments push population-ethics conclusions that are undefended).
- **TESCREAL-bundled politics**. Bucket is not an EA/rationalist venture; it takes the technical arguments without the movement.
- **Silicon-Valley capture**. Much longtermist funding comes from tech billionaires with aligned interests; we cite primaries, not press releases.
