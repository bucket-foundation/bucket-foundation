# art-commentariat/

**Tier: landscape (art criticism + essays + newer-media commentary). Not canon. Parallel to `history-commentariat/`.**

Critics, essayists, and newer-media commentators who shape how art is read. Bucket evaluates each on the same four axes used in `history-commentariat/`:

- **Sourcing rigor** — do they cite primary works, archives, and verifiable facts, or float assertions?
- **Retraction behavior** — when wrong, do they correct on record?
- **Hypothesis vs assertion** — do they flag speculation, or present it as settled?
- **Ad hominem load** — how much of the output is argument vs attack?

No endorsement, no rejection. Index.

## 1. Canon-adjacent critics (primary essays may promote)

These are the critics whose best essays are taught in art-history programs and have survived multiple decades of peer engagement. Individual essays can promote to `bucket-canon/_intake/` if a specific claim is derivable and replicable.

- **Clement Greenberg** — "Avant-Garde and Kitsch" 1939 *Partisan Review*; "Modernist Painting" 1960; *Art and Culture* 1961 (essay collection). Formalist; medium-specificity thesis.
- **Harold Rosenberg** — "The American Action Painters" 1952 *ARTnews*; *The Tradition of the New* 1959.
- **Rosalind Krauss** — *The Originality of the Avant-Garde and Other Modernist Myths* 1985; co-founder *October* journal 1976.
- **Hal Foster** — *The Return of the Real* 1996; *Bad New Days* 2015.
- **T.J. Clark** — *Image of the People: Gustave Courbet and the 1848 Revolution* 1973; *Farewell to an Idea: Episodes from a History of Modernism* 1999. Marxist social history of art.
- **Michael Fried** — "Art and Objecthood" 1967 *Artforum*. The anti-minimalism essay; still argued about.
- **John Berger** — *Ways of Seeing* 1972 (BBC series + book). Cross-referenced to `bucket-canon/_intake/09-art/perception/` as promotion candidate (specific claims about framing + viewer + reproduction).
- **Dave Hickey** — *Air Guitar: Essays on Art and Democracy* 1997; *The Invisible Dragon* 1993.
- **Robert Hughes** — *The Shock of the New* 1980 (BBC series + book); *American Visions* 1997.
- **Arthur Danto** — *The Transfiguration of the Commonplace* 1981; *The Philosophical Disenfranchisement of Art* 1986. Philosophy-adjacent (Hegelian end-of-art thesis).

## 2. Contemporary working critics

- **Jerry Saltz** — *New York* magazine; Pulitzer 2018. High output, high ad hominem load by design; flags speculation reliably.
- **Roberta Smith** — *New York Times*. Low ad hominem, high sourcing.
- **Peter Schjeldahl** (†2022) — *New Yorker* (1998–2022). Essayistic; low retraction history because low falsifiable-claim rate.
- **Ben Davis** — Artnet; *9.5 Theses on Art and Class* 2013. Marxist-adjacent; sourcing rigorous.

## 3. Market-adjacent journalists

- **Georgina Adam** — *Big Bucks* 2014; *Dark Side of the Boom* 2017; long-standing *Art Newspaper* + *FT* columnist. High sourcing rigor on market structure and freeports.
- **Kenny Schachter** — Artnet columnist. Insider/participant; sourcing is personal-network, not archival; explicit about it.
- **Scott Reyburn** — *NYT* + *Art Newspaper* on the auction market.

## 4. Podcast / YouTube / newer-media

- **Great Art Explained** — James Payne (YouTube). Single-work long-form. Sourcing: mainstream art-historical consensus; low retraction history.
- **Nerdwriter1** — Evan Puschak (YouTube). Broad cultural essays including art. Hypothesis-tier framing, usually flagged.
- **The Art Angle** — Artnet podcast. News-cycle driven.
- **Dialogues** — David Zwirner podcast. Gallery-sponsored; flag commercial COI.
- **A Brush with…** — *Art Newspaper* podcast (Ben Luke). Artist long-form interviews.
- **Bad at Sports** — artist-run podcast (Chicago, since 2005). Long archival tail.

## 5. Academic-adjacent

- **Boris Groys** — *Art Power* 2008; *In the Flow* 2016.
- **W.J.T. Mitchell** — *Picture Theory* 1994; *What Do Pictures Want?* 2005.
- **Sven Lütticken** — *Secret Publicity* 2005; *Cultural Revolution* 2017.
- **Claire Bishop** — *Artificial Hells: Participatory Art and the Politics of Spectatorship* 2012; *Radical Museology* 2013.

## Promotion criteria (same as history-commentariat)

An essay promotes to `bucket-canon/_intake/` when:

1. It is a primary essay (not a blog post summarizing a primary essay)
2. The specific claim being promoted is derivable from stated evidence / method
3. A second independent source engages with the claim on its merits (engagement, not celebrity)

Greenberg, Fried, Clark, Danto, and Berger have essays that meet this bar; being on that list does not make the critic's full corpus canon, only the specific essay whose specific claim is promoted.
