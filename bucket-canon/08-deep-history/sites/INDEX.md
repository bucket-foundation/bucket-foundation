# Ancient Sites Index — `08-deep-history/sites/`

Geodetic + chronological index of pre-3000 BCE sites with **primary-literature citations**. Each entry: coordinates (WGS84), dating method + key refs, current consensus date range, significant dating controversies.

> Canon discipline: this index stores citations to primary archaeological reports. Alt-dating hypotheses (e.g. Schoch's Sphinx water-erosion date) are listed as *disputed* with refs to both the hypothesis and the academic rebuttal. Promotion of a disputed date to consensus requires peer-reviewed confirmation.

## Seed entries (pass-1, 2026-04-23) — to be expanded with full citations via DOI pipeline

### Anatolia / Near East
- **Göbekli Tepe** — 37.223°N 38.922°E · ~9600–8200 BCE (PPN A/B) · Schmidt, K. (various 1995–2011 excavation reports, DAI); Dietrich et al. 2012 *Antiquity*. Significance: monumental architecture predating agriculture-and-ceramics. DOI pipeline: Dietrich et al. 2013, Notroff et al. 2014.
- **Karahan Tepe** — 37.100°N 38.917°E · ~9400–8000 BCE · Çelik (various, 2000s–2020s); recent: Karul 2021 *Türk Arkeoloji ve Etnografya Dergisi*.
- **Çatalhöyük** — 37.667°N 32.828°E · ~7100–5700 BCE · Mellaart 1967; Hodder (2006, 2014) multi-volume reports.
- **Nevalı Çori** — submerged (Atatürk Reservoir) · ~8400–8100 BCE · Hauptmann 1993.
- **Jericho (Tell es-Sultan)** — 31.871°N 35.444°E · PPN A tower ~8000 BCE · Kenyon 1957; recent: Nigro 2016.

### Egypt
- **Giza plateau** (pyramid complex) — 29.979°N 31.134°E · Great Pyramid consensus ~2560 BCE (Lehner & Hawass 2017 *Giza and the Pyramids*). Disputed earlier date hypotheses (Schoch, Bauval) → landscape/deep-history-hypotheses.
- **Saqqara** — 29.870°N 31.216°E · Djoser step pyramid ~2670 BCE · Lauer (various).
- **Abu Gorab / sun temples** — 29.897°N 31.187°E · 5th Dynasty.
- **Nabta Playa** — 22.500°N 30.728°E · calendar circle ~4800 BCE · Malville et al. 1998 *Nature*; Brophy 2002 (disputed archaeoastronomy claims → landscape).

### British Isles / Atlantic Europe
- **Stonehenge** — 51.179°N 1.826°W · phases ~3000–1600 BCE · Darvill & Wainwright 2009; Parker Pearson Stonehenge Riverside Project reports.
- **Newgrange (Brú na Bóinne)** — 53.695°N 6.475°W · ~3200 BCE · O'Kelly 1982; winter solstice alignment well-documented primary.
- **Carnac alignments** — 47.583°N 3.083°W · ~4500–3300 BCE · Cassen et al.
- **Orkney (Ness of Brodgar, Maeshowe, Ring of Brodgar)** — 59.0°N 3.2°W · ~3200–2200 BCE · Card (various NoB reports).

### Americas (pre-Columbian / pre-consensus-dates)
- **Teotihuacan** — 19.692°N 98.844°W · ~100 BCE–550 CE · Cowgill 2015; Manzanilla reports. (Post-3000 BCE but foundational city scale.)
- **Tiwanaku** — 16.554°S 68.673°W · ~500 BCE–1000 CE consensus; Posnansky's older-date claims are landscape.
- **Puma Punku** — same coords as Tiwanaku · ~500–600 CE consensus · Vranich 2006, 2016 *Antiquity*. Alt-dating (Posnansky 15000 BCE) → landscape.
- **Caral-Supe** — 10.893°S 77.520°W · ~3000–1800 BCE · Shady Solís et al. 2001 *Science* 292:723 — oldest known complex society in Americas.
- **Monte Verde** — 41.505°S 73.202°W · ~14500 BCE · Dillehay 1997 (peer-reviewed pre-Clovis).

### Oceania / East Asia
- **Nan Madol** — 6.842°N 158.332°E · ~1200–1500 CE consensus (McCoy et al. 2016 *Quat. Res.*).
- **Yonaguni Monument** — 24.437°N 123.011°E · geological consensus: natural formation (Schoch 1999 fieldwork inconclusive; Kimura's "man-made" claim → landscape).
- **Gunung Padang** — 6.995°S 107.056°E · terraced site; Natawidjaja et al. 2023 *Archaeological Prospection* claim deep substructure dates; paper was corrected/partially retracted 2024 → primary-vs-retraction both cited here, current status: disputed.

## Geomagnetic siting hypothesis
The claim that major megalithic sites cluster on geomagnetic anomalies or great-circle alignments (Becker & Hagens, Carl Munck, and others) is **landscape-tier**. Any primary geomagnetic or geodetic survey paper (e.g. INTERMAGNET data) that a canon-tier paper cites is fair game for canon; the synthesis is not.
