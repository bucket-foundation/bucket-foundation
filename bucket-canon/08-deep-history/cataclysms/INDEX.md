# Cataclysm Event Catalog — `08-deep-history/cataclysms/`

Primary literature on known and proposed Holocene / late-Pleistocene cataclysms. Canon discipline: peer-reviewed primary dating + mechanism evidence only. Popular synthesis (Carlson, Hancock-era catastrophism) → `research-landscape/deep-history-hypotheses/`.

## Event classes

### 1. Impact events
- **Chicxulub (K-Pg)** — ~66 Mya · Alvarez et al. 1980 *Science* 208:1095 · methodology template for all subsequent impact-dating work. Hildebrand et al. 1991 (crater identification). Schulte et al. 2010 *Science* 327:1214 (consensus review).
- **Hiawatha crater (Greenland)** — Kjær et al. 2018 *Science Advances* 4:eaar8173 (discovery). Age: Kenny et al. 2022 *Science Advances* dated to ~58 Mya — contradicting earlier speculation it was Younger Dryas-era. Primary refs cite both.
- **Younger Dryas Impact Hypothesis (YDIH)** — proposed ~12,800 years BP. Primaries:
  - Firestone et al. 2007 *PNAS* 104:16016 (original)
  - Kennett et al. 2009 *Science* 323:94 (nanodiamond evidence)
  - Moore et al. 2020 *Scientific Reports* 10:4185 (Abu Hureyra platinum)
  - Wolbach et al. 2018 *J. Geology* 126:165 (global biomass burning)
  - Sweatman 2021 *Earth-Science Reviews* 218:103677 (comprehensive review)
  - **Rebuttals** (also canon — methodological disagreement is canon-tier):
    - Pinter et al. 2011 *Earth-Science Reviews* 106:247
    - Holliday et al. 2014 *J. Quaternary Science* 29:515
    - van Hoesel et al. 2014 *Quat. Sci. Rev.* 83:95
  - Status: **disputed**, impact signature contested but platinum/microspherule/biomass-burn data replicated at multiple sites.

### 2. Solar-proton (Miyake) events
Extreme solar particle events detected via ¹⁴C spikes in tree rings + ¹⁰Be in ice cores.
- **774–775 CE** — Miyake et al. 2012 *Nature* 486:240 (original detection)
- **993–994 CE** — Miyake et al. 2013 *Nature Communications* 4:1748
- **660 BCE** — Park et al. 2017 *PNAS* 114:11442
- **~7176 BCE and ~5259 BCE** — Brehm et al. 2022 *Nature Communications* 13:1196; Miyake et al. 2021 *Geophysical Research Letters* 48:e2020GL091432
- **5480 BCE event** — Miyake et al. 2017 *PNAS* 114:881
Significance: establishes that solar events ~10–100× the 1859 Carrington event have occurred in the Holocene. Tree-ring-based dating precision makes this a methodological linchpin for any claim of radiation-driven historical change.

### 3. Climate transitions
- **Meltwater Pulse 1A** — ~14,600 years BP · rapid sea-level rise · Deschamps et al. 2012 *Nature* 483:559 (Tahiti coral); Liu & Milliman 2004 *J. Ocean Univ.* 3:183.
- **Meltwater Pulse 1B** — ~11,500 years BP · disputed existence · Bard et al. 2010 *Science* 327:1235 (Tahiti data argues weak or absent).
- **Younger Dryas climate event** — ~12,900–11,700 years BP · Alley 2000 *Quat. Sci. Rev.* 19:213 (GISP2 ice-core). Separate from YDIH; the climate event is consensus, its cause is debated.
- **8.2 kiloyear event** — Alley et al. 1997 *Geology* 25:483 · attributed to Lake Agassiz outburst (Barber et al. 1999 *Nature* 400:344).
- **4.2 ka event** — Weiss 2016 (review) *Climate of the Past* 12:1893 · global aridification; added to geological timescale as onset of Meghalayan Age (2018 IUGS ratification).

### 4. Volcanic super-events
- **Toba eruption** — ~74,000 years BP · VEI 8 · Rose & Chesner 1990 *Geology* 18:204; Oppenheimer 2002 *Quat. Sci. Rev.* 21:1593.
- **Campanian Ignimbrite** — ~39,800 years BP · Fedele et al. 2008 *J. Archaeological Science* 35:834; linked to Neanderthal decline (Golovanova et al. 2010 *Current Anthropology* 51:655 — disputed causation).
- **Laacher See** — ~13,000 years BP · Baales et al. 2002 *Quat. Res.* 58:273 (Central Europe ash fall near YD boundary — *not* a YD cause claim unless explicit primary cited).
- **Minoan / Thera (Santorini)** — ~1600 BCE · Friedrich et al. 2006 *Science* 312:548 (olive branch ¹⁴C).

### 5. Geomagnetic excursions
- **Laschamps excursion** — ~42,000 years BP · Nowaczyk et al. 2012 *Earth Planet. Sci. Lett.* 351; **Cooper et al. 2021 *Science* 371:811** ("Adams transitional geomagnetic event" — proposed ecosystem/human correlation; strong methodology, claims contested).
- **Gothenburg / Mono Lake excursions** — multiple primaries.
- Historical reference for geomagnetic-reversal science: Cox, Doell, Dalrymple 1964 *Science* 144:1537.

### 6. Near-miss future events (primary risk literature)
- **Carrington event (1859)** — Carrington 1859 *MNRAS* 20:13; Hodgson 1859 same issue.
- **1989 Quebec event** — Bolduc 2002 *JASTP* 64:1793.
- **2012 solar event (near-miss)** — Baker et al. 2013 *Space Weather* 11:585.
These establish the modern reference for what a 1-in-500-year or 1-in-10,000-year solar event would look like today.
