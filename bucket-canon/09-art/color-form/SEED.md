# color-form — SEED

Primary theoretical texts on color and on visual form. Laws, systems, and
mechanisms — not palette manuals or style guides.

---

## Johann Wolfgang von Goethe — *Zur Farbenlehre* (1810)

- **Edition of record**: Cotta, 1810 (2 vols. + plates). English: Eastlake,
  *Theory of Colours*, 1840 (PD; MIT Press reprint).
- **PD status**: Full PD.
- **Rationale**: Phenomenological color theory grounded in observed
  physiological effects (after-images, colored shadows, contrast). Wrong on
  Newton's spectral claims but foundational for perceptual-color traditions
  from Chevreul and Turner through Itten and Albers.

## Michel Eugène Chevreul — *De la loi du contraste simultané des couleurs*
(1839)

- **Edition of record**: Pitois-Levrault, 1839. English: Martel, *The
  Principles of Harmony and Contrast of Colors*, 1854 (PD).
- **PD status**: Full PD.
- **Rationale**: States the law of simultaneous contrast — a color is
  modified toward the complement of any color adjacent to it. A mechanism
  picked up directly by Delacroix, Seurat, and the Neo-Impressionists, and
  the empirical ancestor of later systematic color-order systems.

## Albert H. Munsell — *A Color Notation* (1905)

- **Edition of record**: Ellis, 1905; ongoing revisions by the Munsell
  Color Company / X-Rite.
- **PD status**: First edition PD.
- **Rationale**: First rigorous perceptually-uniform three-axis color system
  (hue, value, chroma) with a notation adequate for reproduction. Still the
  reference standard for soil science, dermatology, and many industrial
  color specifications.

## Wilhelm Ostwald — Ostwald color system (*Die Farbenfibel*, 1916;
*Der Farbenatlas*, 1917)

- **Edition of record**: Unesma, Leipzig, 1916–1918.
- **PD status**: PD in most jurisdictions.
- **Rationale**: Alternative systematic color order based on full-color /
  white / black ratios. Competing solution to the same problem Munsell
  solved; retained as a primary statement of the color-order program.

## Johannes Itten — *Kunst der Farbe* (1961)

- **Edition of record**: Ravensburger, 1961; abridged as *The Elements of
  Color*, Van Nostrand Reinhold, 1970.
- **PD status**: In copyright.
- **Rationale**: Seven color contrasts (hue, light-dark, cold-warm,
  complementary, simultaneous, saturation, extension) codified as a
  pedagogical system. Direct descendant of Goethe and Chevreul via the
  Bauhaus preliminary course.

## Josef Albers — *Interaction of Color* (1963)

- **Edition of record**: Yale, 1963; Revised and expanded 2006, 2013, 2018.
- **PD status**: In copyright.
- **Rationale**: Demonstrates by controlled studies the relativity of color
  perception — that a color is never seen as itself but in relation to its
  context. A mechanism-level empirical result, not a manual.

## Wassily Kandinsky — *Über das Geistige in der Kunst* (1911)

- **Edition of record**: Piper, Munich, 1911. English: Sadler, *Concerning
  the Spiritual in Art*, 1914 (PD; Dover reprint).
- **PD status**: Full PD.
- **Rationale**: First systematic claim that colors and forms carry
  intrinsic psychological weight independent of representation. The
  theoretical ground for non-objective painting.

## Wassily Kandinsky — *Punkt und Linie zu Fläche* (1926)

- **Edition of record**: Bauhausbücher 9, Albert Langen, 1926. English:
  *Point and Line to Plane*, Guggenheim, 1947.
- **PD status**: German original PD in most jurisdictions.
- **Rationale**: A formal analysis of the elementary visual units — point,
  line, plane — treated as primitives with defined tensional behavior. A
  grammar of pictorial form.

## Paul Klee — *Pädagogisches Skizzenbuch* (1925)

- **Edition of record**: Bauhausbücher 2, Albert Langen, 1925. English:
  Moholy-Nagy, *Pedagogical Sketchbook*, Faber, 1953.
- **PD status**: German original PD in most jurisdictions.
- **Rationale**: Distills Klee's Bauhaus teaching into a graphic primer on
  the dynamics of line, plane, and construction. Compact primary source for
  a theory of pictorial genesis ("taking a line for a walk" is not slogan
  but mechanism).
