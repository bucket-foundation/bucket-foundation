# aesthetic-theory — SEED

Primary texts of aesthetic philosophy. Each entry gives citation,
edition-of-record, public-domain status where known, and a one-paragraph
canon rationale stating the specific mechanism or distinction the text
introduces.

---

## Aristotle — *Poetics* (c. 335 BCE)

- **Edition of record**: Kassel, R. (ed.), *Aristotelis De arte poetica
  liber*, Oxford Classical Texts, 1965. Standard English: Halliwell
  (Loeb, 1995); Janko (Hackett, 1987).
- **PD status**: Greek text PD. Older English translations (Butcher, Bywater)
  PD; modern scholarly translations in copyright.
- **Rationale**: Introduces mimesis, plot-over-character primacy, the
  unity-of-action constraint, the catharsis mechanism, and the first taxonomy
  of genre by object/medium/mode. Every subsequent theory of narrative form
  is either an extension or a rejection of this grammar.

## Longinus — *On the Sublime* (*Peri Hupsous*, 1st c. CE, attributed)

- **Edition of record**: Russell, D. A. (ed.), Oxford, 1964. Loeb
  Classical Library (Fyfe, rev. Russell, 1995).
- **PD status**: Greek PD. Smith 1739 English PD.
- **Rationale**: Primary theoretical treatment of the sublime as an aesthetic
  category distinct from beauty — the five sources of elevated style, the
  doctrine of *ekstasis* in the audience. Upstream of Burke and Kant.

## Horace — *Ars Poetica* (*Epistula ad Pisones*, c. 19 BCE)

- **Edition of record**: Brink, C. O., *Horace on Poetry*, Cambridge
  (3 vols., 1963–1982). Loeb (Fairclough).
- **PD status**: Latin PD; older English translations PD.
- **Rationale**: Codifies *decorum*, *ut pictura poesis*, and the aim
  *prodesse et delectare*. Reference point for eighteenth-century neoclassical
  poetics and for the painting/poetry comparison Lessing later attacks.

## Edmund Burke — *A Philosophical Enquiry into the Origin of Our Ideas of
the Sublime and Beautiful* (1757; rev. 1759)

- **Edition of record**: Boulton, J. T. (ed.), Notre Dame, 1968 (reprinted
  Routledge).
- **PD status**: Full PD.
- **Rationale**: First empirical-psychological separation of the sublime
  (terror, vastness, obscurity) from the beautiful (smallness, smoothness,
  gradual variation) as distinct affective mechanisms. Direct input to Kant's
  third *Kritik*.

## Gotthold Ephraim Lessing — *Laokoon: oder über die Grenzen der Malerei
und Poesie* (1766)

- **Edition of record**: Göpfert, H. (ed.), *Werke*, Hanser, vol. 6.
  English: Frothingham (PD); McCormick (Johns Hopkins, 1984).
- **PD status**: German PD; Frothingham 1874 translation PD.
- **Rationale**: Foundational argument that painting and poetry have
  distinct *proper objects* — bodies-in-space vs. actions-in-time — breaking
  *ut pictura poesis*. A mechanism-level claim about medium specificity.

## Immanuel Kant — *Kritik der Urteilskraft* (1790)

- **Edition of record**: Akademie-Ausgabe vol. 5. English: Guyer &
  Matthews, *Critique of the Power of Judgment*, Cambridge, 2000 (current
  academic standard). Meredith (1952) still widely cited.
- **PD status**: German PD; Bernard 1892, Meredith 1911 English PD; Guyer &
  Matthews in copyright.
- **Rationale**: Core text. The four moments of the judgment of taste
  (disinterested, universal without concept, purposive without purpose,
  necessary), the distinction of the beautiful from the sublime
  (mathematical vs. dynamical), and the doctrine of free play of
  imagination and understanding. Every subsequent analytic aesthetics
  either operates inside this frame or defines itself against it.

## Friedrich Schiller — *Über die ästhetische Erziehung des Menschen in
einer Reihe von Briefen* (1795)

- **Edition of record**: Nationalausgabe vol. 20. English: Wilkinson &
  Willoughby, Oxford, 1967 (bilingual).
- **PD status**: German PD; older English PD.
- **Rationale**: The *Spieltrieb* (play drive) as the mechanism reconciling
  sensuous drive and form drive. First post-Kantian claim that aesthetic
  experience does specific *anthropological* work, not just judgment work.

## Arthur Schopenhauer — *Die Welt als Wille und Vorstellung*, Book III
(1819; expanded 1844)

- **Edition of record**: Hübscher, *Sämtliche Werke*. English: Payne
  (Dover, 1966); Norman, Welchman & Janaway (Cambridge, 2010).
- **PD status**: German PD; Haldane & Kemp PD.
- **Rationale**: Art as momentary release from the will through contemplation
  of Platonic Ideas; the ranking of arts culminating in music as the direct
  objectification of will rather than representation of Ideas. Direct input
  to Nietzsche and Wagner.

## G. W. F. Hegel — *Vorlesungen über die Ästhetik* (delivered 1820s, pub.
Hotho 1835)

- **Edition of record**: Suhrkamp Werkausgabe vols. 13–15. English: Knox,
  *Aesthetics: Lectures on Fine Art*, Oxford, 1975 (2 vols.).
- **PD status**: German PD; Knox translation in copyright.
- **Rationale**: Art as the sensuous appearance of the Idea; the
  symbolic/classical/romantic periodization as stages of spirit's adequacy to
  its medium; the "end of art" thesis as the structural claim that art's
  highest task is surpassed by philosophy. A theoretical frame, not a
  historical narrative, despite the lecture format.

## Friedrich Nietzsche — *Die Geburt der Tragödie aus dem Geiste der Musik*
(1872)

- **Edition of record**: Colli & Montinari, KGW III/1. English: Kaufmann
  (Random House, 1967).
- **PD status**: German PD; Haussmann 1909 English PD.
- **Rationale**: The Apollonian/Dionysian opposition as a primary pair of
  art-making principles (individuation vs. dissolution). A diagnostic lens
  still load-bearing for theories of form and intoxication in art.

## Benedetto Croce — *Estetica come scienza dell'espressione e linguistica
generale* (1902)

- **Edition of record**: Laterza, 1902. English: Ainslie, *Aesthetic*,
  Macmillan, 1909.
- **PD status**: Italian PD; Ainslie translation PD.
- **Rationale**: Art as *intuition-expression*; denial that art is a
  physical object or external technique. A strong identity claim that
  remains the reference position for expression theories.

## R. G. Collingwood — *The Principles of Art* (1938)

- **Edition of record**: Oxford, 1938.
- **PD status**: In copyright (UK life+70).
- **Rationale**: Refines the expression thesis by distinguishing art proper
  from craft, amusement, and magic; the "imaginative expression of emotion"
  as the identifying mechanism. Contemporary touchstone in analytic
  aesthetics.

## Nelson Goodman — *Languages of Art: An Approach to a Theory of Symbols*
(1968; 2nd ed. 1976)

- **Edition of record**: Hackett, 2nd ed., 1976.
- **PD status**: In copyright.
- **Rationale**: The autographic/allographic distinction, notationality, and
  symptom-of-the-aesthetic criteria. Replaces question "what is art?" with
  "when is art?" and grounds it in symbol-system mechanics.

## Arthur Danto — *The Transfiguration of the Commonplace* (1981)

- **Edition of record**: Harvard, 1981.
- **PD status**: In copyright.
- **Rationale**: The indiscernibles argument (Warhol's *Brillo Box* vs. a
  shipping carton) forces the conclusion that art-identity is not
  perceptual but interpretive — a canonical move in the institutional/
  intentional turn.

## Roger Scruton — *Beauty* (2009)

- **Status**: FLAGGED — living-author (deceased 2020), single monograph,
  higher bar. Included as a recent statement of a judgment-realist position
  answering Danto and Goodman. Candidate, not confirmed canon; retain
  pending second-pass review.
- **Edition of record**: Oxford, 2009; expanded as *Beauty: A Very Short
  Introduction*, Oxford, 2011.
- **PD status**: In copyright.
- **Rationale (provisional)**: Argues beauty is a genuine evaluative category
  grounded in judgment, against the institutional/interpretive turn.
  Include one work only; no career summary.
