# perception — SEED

Primary texts on the perceptual and cognitive foundations of seeing art.
These are not art history; they are theories of how images, forms, and
pictorial conventions work on the eye and the mind.

---

## Rudolf Arnheim — *Art and Visual Perception: A Psychology of the Creative
Eye* (1954; rev. ed. 1974)

- **Edition of record**: University of California Press, new version, 1974.
- **PD status**: In copyright.
- **Rationale**: Applies Gestalt principles (balance, shape, form, space,
  light, color, movement, dynamics, expression) as law-like constraints on
  pictorial composition. Mechanism-level, not descriptive.

## Rudolf Arnheim — *Visual Thinking* (1969)

- **Edition of record**: University of California Press, 1969.
- **PD status**: In copyright.
- **Rationale**: Argues perception is already cognitive — that seeing
  performs the work usually credited to abstract thought. Foundational to
  later work on diagrammatic and pictorial reasoning.

## E. H. Gombrich — *Art and Illusion: A Study in the Psychology of
Pictorial Representation* (1960)

- **Edition of record**: Phaidon / Bollingen Series XXXV:5; current Phaidon
  Millennium Edition (2000).
- **PD status**: In copyright.
- **Rationale**: The "schema and correction" mechanism — artists do not copy
  appearances but modify inherited schemata under the pressure of the motif.
  Directly grounds the cognitive-psychology turn in art history.

## Erwin Panofsky — *Studies in Iconology: Humanistic Themes in the Art of
the Renaissance* (1939)

- **Edition of record**: Oxford, 1939; Harper Torchbooks reissue.
- **PD status**: In copyright.
- **Rationale**: Codifies the three-level method (pre-iconographic description,
  iconographic analysis, iconological interpretation). A primary statement of
  interpretive method, not a survey.

## Erwin Panofsky — *Meaning in the Visual Arts* (1955)

- **Edition of record**: Doubleday Anchor, 1955.
- **PD status**: In copyright.
- **Rationale**: Collects the programmatic essays ("Iconography and
  Iconology", "The History of the Theory of Human Proportions", "Perspective
  as Symbolic Form" — the last as separate reference). Retained for the
  methodological essays, not for the period studies.

## Heinrich Wölfflin — *Kunstgeschichtliche Grundbegriffe* (1915)

- **Edition of record**: Schwabe, 1915; current: *Principles of Art History*,
  tr. Hottinger, Dover; new tr. Blower, Getty, 2015.
- **PD status**: German PD; Hottinger 1932 translation PD in many
  jurisdictions.
- **Rationale**: The five polar pairs (linear/painterly, plane/recession,
  closed/open form, multiplicity/unity, clearness/unclearness) as formal
  categories for analysis independent of content. The founding text of
  formal analysis.

## Michael Baxandall — *Painting and Experience in Fifteenth-Century Italy*
(1972)

- **Edition of record**: Oxford, 1972; 2nd ed. 1988.
- **PD status**: In copyright.
- **Rationale**: The *period eye* thesis — that pictorial conventions are
  intelligible only against the cognitive equipment of their original
  audience (dancing, gauging barrels, preaching). A mechanism for historical
  perception.

## John Berger — *Ways of Seeing* (1972)

- **Status**: FLAGGED — dual status. Primarily landscape commentariat /
  ideological reading. Retained here for the reproduction-and-aura argument
  (extending Benjamin) and the male-gaze mechanism (chapters 2 and 3), which
  have become reference points. Include narrowly for those two mechanisms,
  not as general method.
- **Edition of record**: BBC / Penguin, 1972.
- **PD status**: In copyright.
