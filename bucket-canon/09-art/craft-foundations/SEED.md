# craft-foundations — SEED

Primary treatises on the craft, material, and proportional foundations of
painting and architecture. These are *how-to-make* texts at the theoretical
level — statements of principle, not recipe collections.

---

## Vitruvius — *De architectura* (c. 15 BCE)

- **Edition of record**: Krohn (Teubner, 1912); Fensterbusch (bilingual,
  WBG, 1964). English: Morgan (Harvard, 1914, PD); Rowland & Howe
  (Cambridge, 1999).
- **PD status**: Latin PD; Morgan translation PD.
- **Rationale**: Only complete architectural treatise surviving from
  antiquity. States the *firmitas, utilitas, venustas* triad, the Vitruvian
  proportional system, and the orders. Reference text for every subsequent
  Western architectural theory.

## Cennino Cennini — *Il libro dell'arte* (c. 1400)

- **Edition of record**: Frezzato (ed.), Neri Pozza, 2003. English:
  Thompson, *The Craftsman's Handbook*, Yale, 1933 (Dover reprint, PD).
- **PD status**: Italian PD; Thompson translation PD.
- **Rationale**: First surviving workshop treatise on late-medieval / early
  Renaissance painting technique — grounds, pigments, tempera, gilding,
  fresco. Primary source on the material basis of Trecento painting.

## Leon Battista Alberti — *De pictura* (1435; Italian *Della pittura*
1436)

- **Edition of record**: Grayson, *On Painting*, Phaidon, 1972; Sinisgalli
  (Cambridge, 2011) with Latin text.
- **PD status**: Latin and Italian PD; older English PD.
- **Rationale**: First theoretical statement of linear perspective (the
  *costruzione legittima*), the definition of *istoria* as the highest goal
  of painting, and the mathematical grounding of picture construction.

## Leon Battista Alberti — *De re aedificatoria* (completed c. 1452,
printed 1485)

- **Edition of record**: Orlandi & Portoghesi (Il Polifilo, 1966).
  English: Rykwert, Leach & Tavernor (MIT, 1988).
- **PD status**: Latin PD; Leoni 1726 English PD.
- **Rationale**: The first modern treatise on architecture, re-stating and
  extending Vitruvius. Introduces *concinnitas* — the reasoned harmony of
  parts — as the defining architectural criterion.

## Leonardo da Vinci — notebooks (compiled)

- **Edition of record**: Richter, *The Literary Works of Leonardo da
  Vinci*, 1883 (PD); Pedretti commentary (2 vols., Phaidon, 1977);
  *Trattato della pittura* / *Treatise on Painting* as compiled by Melzi
  (Codex Urbinas Latinus 1270), ed. McMahon, Princeton, 1956.
- **PD status**: Leonardo's Italian PD; Richter and MacCurdy translations
  PD.
- **Rationale**: Primary source for Renaissance theory of painting treated
  as a science (*scienza*) — sfumato, aerial perspective, the comparison of
  the arts (*paragone*), anatomy for artists. Retained as compilations; the
  *Trattato* is the single most important edited witness.

## Albrecht Dürer — *Underweysung der Messung* (1525)

- **Edition of record**: Nuremberg, 1525. English: Strauss, *The Painter's
  Manual*, Abaris, 1977.
- **PD status**: German PD.
- **Rationale**: First geometry book in German aimed at artists; introduces
  constructions for curves, regular polygons, perspective, and lettering
  (Roman and Fraktur). A primary source on mathematized craft.

## Albrecht Dürer — *Vier Bücher von menschlicher Proportion* (1528)

- **Edition of record**: Nuremberg, 1528. English: Strauss, *The Human
  Figure*, Dover, 1972.
- **PD status**: German PD.
- **Rationale**: Systematic study of human proportions with multiple types
  rather than a single ideal canon. The first published attempt at an
  empirical, variational theory of proportion.

## Andrea Palladio — *I quattro libri dell'architettura* (1570)

- **Edition of record**: Venice, 1570. English: Tavernor & Schofield,
  *The Four Books on Architecture*, MIT, 1997; Ware 1738 (PD).
- **PD status**: Italian PD; Ware English PD.
- **Rationale**: The most influential post-antique architectural treatise.
  States Palladio's proportional rules, the orders as codified after
  Vignola, and the villa/palace/church types via his own built examples.

## Andrea Pozzo — *Perspectiva Pictorum et Architectorum* (1693–1700)

- **Edition of record**: Rome, 1693 (vol. I), 1700 (vol. II). English:
  *Rules and Examples of Perspective*, Sturt, 1707 (PD; Dover reprint).
- **PD status**: Full PD.
- **Rationale**: Exhaustive practical and theoretical treatment of
  quadratura and illusionistic ceiling painting — the most rigorous primary
  source on projective perspective as applied to built surfaces.

## John Ruskin — *The Seven Lamps of Architecture* (1849)

- **Edition of record**: Smith, Elder & Co., 1849; Cook & Wedderburn,
  *Works*, vol. 8, 1903.
- **PD status**: Full PD.
- **Rationale**: States seven principles (Sacrifice, Truth, Power, Beauty,
  Life, Memory, Obedience) as moral-structural criteria for building. A
  normative theory of architecture, not a style history.

## John Ruskin — *The Stones of Venice* (1851–53)

- **Edition of record**: Smith, Elder & Co., 1851–53; *Works* vols. 9–11.
- **PD status**: Full PD.
- **Rationale**: Retained for the theoretical chapters — above all "The
  Nature of Gothic" (vol. II ch. VI) — which state the craft-labor
  diagnosis of ornament and worker-dignity that founds the Arts and Crafts
  position. Not the Venetian history chapters.

## Eugène Viollet-le-Duc — *Dictionnaire raisonné de l'architecture
française du XIe au XVIe siècle* (1854–68)

- **Edition of record**: Bance / Morel, 10 vols., 1854–1868.
- **PD status**: Full PD.
- **Rationale**: The rationalist theory of Gothic — structure as
  determinant of form, ornament as expression of statics. Retained as a
  primary theoretical source on structural rationalism, not as reference
  encyclopedia.
