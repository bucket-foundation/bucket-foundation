# design — DOSSIER

Primary texts of design theory from the Bauhaus lineage through
late-twentieth-century information-design and HCI theory. Mechanism-first:
each entry states the specific principle or construct the work contributes.

---

## Walter Gropius — *Programm des Staatlichen Bauhauses in Weimar* (Bauhaus
Manifesto) (1919)

- **Edition of record**: Bauhaus, Weimar, April 1919 (Feininger cover).
  Reproduced in Wingler, *The Bauhaus*, MIT, 1969.
- **PD status**: German original PD.
- **Contribution**: States the unification of fine art, craft, and
  architecture under a single educational program — the founding doctrine
  of the modern design-school.

## László Moholy-Nagy — *Von Material zu Architektur* (1929)

- **Edition of record**: Bauhausbücher 14, Albert Langen, 1929. English
  revision: *The New Vision*, 4th ed., Wittenborn, 1947.
- **PD status**: German original PD in most jurisdictions; later editions
  in copyright.
- **Contribution**: Systematic pedagogy of materials, volume, space, and
  motion as the sequence of design primitives — the theoretical companion
  to the Bauhaus *Vorkurs*.

## László Moholy-Nagy — *Vision in Motion* (1947)

- **Edition of record**: Paul Theobald, Chicago, 1947.
- **PD status**: In copyright in most jurisdictions.
- **Contribution**: Extends the earlier framework to time-based media and
  integrated design; argues design is a universal problem-solving method.

## Johannes Itten — *Design and Form: The Basic Course at the Bauhaus*
(1963; rev. 1975)

- **Edition of record**: Otto Maier / Ravensburger, 1963. English: Van
  Nostrand Reinhold, 1964, rev. 1975.
- **PD status**: In copyright.
- **Contribution**: Primary record of the Bauhaus preliminary course — the
  contrast-pair pedagogy (light/dark, material/texture, form, rhythm) as a
  transferable method for training perception.

## György Kepes — *Language of Vision* (1944)

- **Edition of record**: Paul Theobald, Chicago, 1944.
- **PD status**: PD status ambiguous; treat as in copyright.
- **Contribution**: First English-language codification of Bauhaus
  visual-grammar principles (plastic organization, representation,
  communication) as a unified "language" — direct ancestor of later
  visual-literacy and graphic-design theory.

## Victor Papanek — *Design for the Real World: Human Ecology and Social
Change* (1971; rev. 1984)

- **Edition of record**: Pantheon, 1971; 2nd ed. Academy Chicago, 1984.
- **PD status**: In copyright.
- **Contribution**: Reframes design's object from consumer goods to human
  need; introduces the function-complex (method, use, need, telesis,
  association, aesthetics) as a normative evaluation tool.

## Dieter Rams — Ten Principles for Good Design (c. late 1970s / early
1980s)

- **Primary source**: Rams's own lectures and interviews as published by
  Vitsoe; earliest cited formulation in the 1984 IDSA talk "Omit the
  Unimportant". Collected authoritatively in Lovell, *Dieter Rams: As Little
  Design as Possible*, Phaidon, 2011 (foreword and quoted lectures by Rams).
- **PD status**: In copyright.
- **Contribution**: The ten criteria (innovative, useful, aesthetic,
  understandable, unobtrusive, honest, long-lasting, thorough down to the
  last detail, environmentally-friendly, as little design as possible) as
  an operational evaluation checklist. Cite Rams's primary statements, not
  the Phaidon compilation as such.

## Christopher Alexander — *Notes on the Synthesis of Form* (1964)

- **Edition of record**: Harvard, 1964.
- **PD status**: In copyright.
- **Contribution**: Formalizes design as a decomposition of the
  requirement set into a tree of sub-problems; introduces misfit variables
  as the unit of analysis. Early rigorous attempt at a theory of the
  design process.

## Christopher Alexander, Sara Ishikawa, Murray Silverstein et al. — *A
Pattern Language: Towns, Buildings, Construction* (1977)

- **Edition of record**: Oxford, 1977.
- **PD status**: In copyright.
- **Contribution**: 253 named patterns with context–problem–solution
  structure at multiple scales. The pattern-language form itself is the
  contribution — it was carried into software by Gamma et al. and into HCI.

## Christopher Alexander — *The Timeless Way of Building* (1979)

- **Edition of record**: Oxford, 1979.
- **PD status**: In copyright.
- **Contribution**: Theoretical companion to *A Pattern Language*; states
  the *quality without a name* thesis and the generative role of patterns.

## Edward R. Tufte — *The Visual Display of Quantitative Information*
(1983; 2nd ed. 2001)

- **Status**: Living author. Include this single work as primary canon.
- **Edition of record**: Graphics Press, 1st ed. 1983; 2nd ed. 2001.
- **PD status**: In copyright.
- **Contribution**: Data-ink ratio, chartjunk, small multiples, and the
  Lie Factor as quantitative evaluation criteria for statistical graphics.

## Edward R. Tufte — *Envisioning Information* (1990)

- **Status**: Living author, second work. Retained for the specific
  mechanism below only; no career summary.
- **Edition of record**: Graphics Press, 1990.
- **PD status**: In copyright.
- **Contribution**: *Escaping flatland* — the repertoire of techniques
  (layering and separation, small multiples, micro/macro readings, color)
  for displaying high-dimensional information in two dimensions.

## Edward R. Tufte — *Visual Explanations: Images and Quantities, Evidence
and Narrative* (1997)

- **Status**: FLAGGED — third Tufte volume. Retained only for the
  evidence-presentation mechanism (the Challenger and cholera case studies
  as methodological exemplars); consolidate on second pass if overlap with
  the first two is excessive.
- **Edition of record**: Graphics Press, 1997.
- **PD status**: In copyright.

## Donald A. Norman — *The Psychology of Everyday Things* (1988) / *The
Design of Everyday Things* (retitled 1990; rev. 2013)

- **Status**: Living author. Single primary work.
- **Edition of record**: Basic Books, 1988; Revised and Expanded, 2013.
- **PD status**: In copyright.
- **Contribution**: Affordances, signifiers, mappings, feedback, and the
  seven stages of action as an operational model of human–artifact
  interaction. Founding vocabulary of modern HCI and product design.

## Bruno Munari — *Arte come mestiere* (1966) / *Design as Art* (1971)

- **Edition of record**: Laterza, 1966. English: Penguin, 1971.
- **PD status**: In copyright.
- **Contribution**: Essays arguing the designer as problem-solver rather
  than artist-personality; the designed object evaluated on clarity,
  economy, and communicative function.

## Paul Rand — *Thoughts on Design* (1947; repr. Chronicle, 2014)

- **Edition of record**: Wittenborn & Co., 1947; facsimile Chronicle, 2014.
- **PD status**: First edition PD status ambiguous; treat as in copyright.
- **Contribution**: First book-length statement of a modernist theory of
  graphic design: symbol, juxtaposition, the beautiful/useful pair. A
  primary source on graphic design as discipline.

## Paul Rand — *A Designer's Art* (1985)

- **Edition of record**: Yale, 1985; reissued 2000.
- **PD status**: In copyright.
- **Contribution**: Mature restatement of Rand's theory, with the logo /
  trademark theory (simplicity, memorability, appropriateness) as
  operational criteria.

## John Maeda — *The Laws of Simplicity* (2006)

- **Status**: FLAGGED — borderline canon. Popular-format synthesis rather
  than primary theoretical contribution. Retain pending second-pass
  evaluation; likely demote to landscape if a specific mechanism beyond
  aphorism is not identified.
- **Edition of record**: MIT Press, 2006.
- **PD status**: In copyright.
