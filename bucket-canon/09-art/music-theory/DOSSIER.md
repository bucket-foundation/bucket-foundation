# music-theory — DOSSIER

Primary theoretical texts on music. Mechanism and grammar only — no
performance pedagogy, no style history. Helmholtz 1863 is the linchpin and
is cross-referenced from `02-physics/acoustics`.

**Cross-reference**: `../../02-physics/acoustics/` — the physics of vibrating
strings, air columns, partials, and consonance provides the acoustic
substrate for Pythagorean-tradition ratios and for Helmholtz's
psycho-acoustic account of consonance.

---

## Pythagoras (6th c. BCE)

- **Status**: No writings survive. Cited via the Pythagorean tradition
  witnesses below.
- **Primary witnesses**:
  - Philolaus of Croton — fragments (Diels-Kranz 44 B). Edition: Huffman,
    *Philolaus of Croton: Pythagorean and Presocratic*, Cambridge, 1993.
  - Nicomachus of Gerasa — *Harmonikon Encheiridion* (2nd c. CE). Edition:
    Jan, *Musici Scriptores Graeci*, Teubner, 1895 (PD). English: Levin,
    *The Manual of Harmonics of Nicomachus the Pythagorean*, Phanes, 1994.
- **Rationale**: Primary source for the ratio theory of consonance —
  octave 2:1, fifth 3:2, fourth 4:3 — tying musical intervals to integer
  proportions on a monochord. The foundational empirical claim that musical
  pitch obeys mathematical law.

## Aristoxenus of Tarentum — *Elementa harmonica* (c. 320 BCE)

- **Edition of record**: Da Rios, Rome, 1954. English: Macran, Oxford,
  1902 (PD).
- **PD status**: Greek PD; Macran translation PD.
- **Rationale**: Opposes the Pythagoreans by grounding music theory in the
  trained ear rather than ratios. States the genera (diatonic, chromatic,
  enharmonic) and the tetrachord-based analysis of scale. The other pole of
  ancient music theory, kept in tension with Pythagoras ever since.

## Boethius — *De institutione musica* (c. 500 CE)

- **Edition of record**: Friedlein, Teubner, 1867 (PD). English: Bower,
  *Fundamentals of Music*, Yale, 1989.
- **PD status**: Latin PD.
- **Rationale**: Transmits Greek theory (Nicomachus, Ptolemy) to the
  Latin West with the *musica mundana / humana / instrumentalis* framework.
  The standard medieval music-theory textbook for a millennium.

## Guido of Arezzo — *Micrologus* (c. 1026)

- **Edition of record**: Smits van Waesberghe, *Corpus Scriptorum de
  Musica* 4, American Institute of Musicology, 1955. English: Babb in
  *Hucbald, Guido, and John on Music*, Yale, 1978.
- **PD status**: Latin PD.
- **Rationale**: Introduces staff notation and the hexachordal
  solmization system (*ut re mi fa sol la*). A primary source on the
  notational and pedagogical machinery that made Western polyphony
  possible.

## Gioseffo Zarlino — *Le istitutioni harmoniche* (1558)

- **Edition of record**: Venice, 1558. English (Part III): Marco,
  *The Art of Counterpoint*, Yale, 1968; (Part IV): Cohen, *On the Modes*,
  Yale, 1983.
- **PD status**: Italian PD.
- **Rationale**: First systematic theory of consonance grounded in the
  *senario* (1:2:3:4:5:6), codifying the major and minor triads as
  theoretical objects. The reference text on late-Renaissance counterpoint
  and the just-intonation tradition.

## Jean-Philippe Rameau — *Traité de l'harmonie réduite à ses principes
naturels* (1722)

- **Edition of record**: Ballard, Paris, 1722. English: Gossett, *Treatise
  on Harmony*, Dover, 1971.
- **PD status**: French PD.
- **Rationale**: Foundational text of tonal harmony. Introduces the
  *basse fondamentale* (fundamental bass), chord inversion, and the
  derivation of the chord from a root — the conceptual apparatus
  underlying all subsequent functional-harmony theory.

## Jean-Philippe Rameau — *Génération harmonique* (1737)

- **Edition of record**: Prault fils, Paris, 1737. English: Hayes,
  Stanford dissertation, 1968.
- **PD status**: French PD.
- **Rationale**: Rameau's re-grounding of harmony in the overtone series
  (*corps sonore*). Completes the move to a physically-grounded tonal
  theory.

## Johann Philipp Kirnberger — *Die Kunst des reinen Satzes in der Musik*
(1771–79)

- **Edition of record**: Decker & Hartung, Berlin, 1771–1779. English:
  Beach & Thym, *The Art of Strict Musical Composition*, Yale, 1982.
- **PD status**: German PD.
- **Rationale**: Bach-lineage systematization of strict counterpoint and
  harmony. Primary source for the pre-Riemann German theory tradition and
  for the distinction between essential and non-essential dissonance.

## François-Joseph Fétis — *Traité complet de la théorie et de la pratique
de l'harmonie* (1844)

- **Edition of record**: Brandus, Paris, 1844.
- **PD status**: French PD.
- **Rationale**: Introduces the historicized *ordres* of tonality (unitonic,
  transitonic, pluritonic, omnitonic). A primary statement of the view that
  tonal systems are historically contingent — upstream of twentieth-century
  chromatic theory.

## Hermann von Helmholtz — *Die Lehre von den Tonempfindungen als
physiologische Grundlage für die Theorie der Musik* (1863; 4th ed. 1877)

- **Edition of record**: Vieweg, Braunschweig, 1863; 4th ed. 1877.
  English: Ellis, *On the Sensations of Tone*, 1875; 2nd English ed. 1885
  (PD; Dover reprint).
- **PD status**: Full PD (German and Ellis English).
- **Rationale**: LINCHPIN. Grounds consonance and dissonance in the
  physics of partials and the physiology of the cochlea (beats, roughness,
  combination tones). Simultaneously the primary acoustics text and a
  primary music-theory text — cross-referenced from
  `02-physics/acoustics/`. Reshaped every subsequent theory of consonance.

## Hugo Riemann — *Handbuch der Harmonielehre* (1887)

- **Edition of record**: Breitkopf & Härtel, Leipzig, 1887 (and later eds.).
- **PD status**: German PD.
- **Rationale**: Functional harmony codified — Tonic, Dominant,
  Subdominant as the three primary functions, with transformations
  (parallel, Leittonwechsel, relative) linking them. The theoretical
  vocabulary revived in late-twentieth-century neo-Riemannian theory.

## Heinrich Schenker — *Der freie Satz* (1935)

- **Edition of record**: Universal Edition, Vienna, 1935; 2nd ed.
  (Jonas) 1956. English: Oster, *Free Composition*, Longman, 1979.
- **PD status**: German original PD in some jurisdictions (author d. 1935).
- **Rationale**: The *Ursatz* — a background contrapuntal structure
  underlying all tonal works — together with the prolongation technique
  and layered-reduction method (background / middleground / foreground).
  The most influential twentieth-century theory of tonal structure.

## Paul Hindemith — *Unterweisung im Tonsatz* I (1937) / *The Craft of
Musical Composition* (1942)

- **Edition of record**: Schott, Mainz, 1937 (Band I). English: Mendel,
  Associated Music Publishers, 1942; rev. 1945.
- **PD status**: In copyright in most jurisdictions.
- **Rationale**: An overtone-series-based theory of chord roots and
  harmonic fluctuation that extends tonal theory to post-tonal materials.
  A rare primary statement of a non-serialist but post-functional harmonic
  system.

## Olivier Messiaen — *Technique de mon langage musical* (1944)

- **Edition of record**: Leduc, Paris, 1944 (2 vols.). English: Satterfield,
  Leduc, 1956.
- **PD status**: In copyright.
- **Rationale**: Messiaen's own account of his techniques: the modes of
  limited transposition, non-retrogradable rhythms, and added values.
  Primary source for a twentieth-century compositional system with a
  mechanism-level grammar.

## Iannis Xenakis — *Musiques formelles* (1963) / *Formalized Music:
Thought and Mathematics in Composition* (1971; rev. 1992)

- **Edition of record**: *La Revue Musicale*, 1963; Indiana UP 1971;
  Pendragon Press rev. ed. 1992.
- **PD status**: In copyright.
- **Rationale**: States stochastic, Markov-chain, game-theoretic, and
  sieve-theoretic compositional methods. A primary source for
  mathematically-grounded composition outside the serial tradition.

## Allen Forte — *The Structure of Atonal Music* (1973)

- **Edition of record**: Yale, 1973.
- **PD status**: In copyright.
- **Rationale**: Pitch-class set theory — the systematic analytic
  apparatus (set classes, interval vectors, the prime-form algorithm) for
  non-tonal twentieth-century music. The reference method for atonal
  analysis.

## Fred Lerdahl & Ray Jackendoff — *A Generative Theory of Tonal Music*
(1983)

- **Edition of record**: MIT Press, 1983.
- **PD status**: In copyright.
- **Rationale**: Applies generative-grammar methodology to tonal music:
  grouping structure, metrical structure, time-span reduction,
  prolongational reduction, with explicit well-formedness and preference
  rules. A primary cognitive-science-grounded theory of tonal
  comprehension.
