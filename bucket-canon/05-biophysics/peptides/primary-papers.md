# Peptides — Primary Papers

Annotated mechanistic bibliography, organized by compound family. Mechanism only. See `SEED.md` for discipline.

---

## 1. Khavinson bioregulators (epitalon, cortexin)

**Flag — Russian-institute provenance.** Most primary data come from the St. Petersburg Institute of Bioregulation and Gerontology. English translations exist in *Bulletin of Experimental Biology and Medicine* (Springer) and *Neuroendocrinology Letters*; independent Western replication is thin.

- **Khavinson VK. 2002.** "Peptides and ageing." *Bull Exp Biol Med* 133(1):1. English-language foundational review of the short-peptide bioregulator program.
- **Khavinson VK, Morozov VG. 2003.** *Neuroendocrinol Lett* 24(3-4):233. Proposed mechanism of epitalon (Ala-Glu-Asp-Gly) on pineal function.
- **Khavinson VK, Bondarev IE, Butyugov AA. 2003.** *Bull Exp Biol Med* 135(6):590. Report of telomerase induction by epitalon in human somatic cell culture. Load-bearing primary for the "short peptide → gene expression" claim; replication status outside Khavinson's group remains the key open question.
- **Anisimov VN, Khavinson VK, et al.** Multiple rodent-longevity papers co-administering melatonin + epitalon. Cited as a body of work; individual DOIs harvested via pipeline.

## 2. BPC-157 (body protection compound, pentadecapeptide)

**Flag — single-lab dominance.** The overwhelming majority of primary BPC-157 literature originates from P. Sikiric and the Zagreb group (Seiwerth, Pavlov, et al.). Independent replication exists but is disproportionately small relative to claims.

- **Sikiric P, et al. 1993.** *J Physiol Paris* 87(5):313. Original characterization as a gastric-juice-derived pentadecapeptide with cytoprotective activity.
- **Sikiric P, Seiwerth S, et al. 2018.** *Curr Neuropharmacol* 16(8):1224. Mechanistic review: NO system modulation, VEGFR2 activation, angiogenesis in rat models.
- Sikiric/Seiwerth rat-model series — angiogenesis, tendon/ligament healing, dopaminergic modulation. Indexed as a corpus; single-lab dominance flag applies to all.

## 3. MOTS-c (mitochondrial-derived peptide)

- **Lee C, Zeng J, Drew BG, … Cohen P. 2015.** "The mitochondrial-derived peptide MOTS-c promotes metabolic homeostasis and reduces obesity and insulin resistance." *Cell Metab* 21(3):443. DOI: 10.1016/j.cmet.2015.02.009. **Core paper.** Identifies 16-aa peptide encoded within mitochondrial 12S rRNA; AMPK activation; folate-methionine pathway.
- **Cobb LJ, et al. 2016.** *Aging* 8(4):796. MOTS-c levels decline with age; functional implications.
- **Merry TL, et al. 2020.** *Aging Cell* 19:e13141. MOTS-c and exercise-induced metabolic adaptation.
- **Kim KH, et al. 2018.** *Cell Metab* 28(3):516. MOTS-c as retrograde mito→nucleus signaling molecule under metabolic stress.

## 4. GHK-Cu (glycyl-histidyl-lysine copper complex)

- **Pickart L, Thaler MM. 1973.** *FEBS Lett* 34:1. Original isolation of GHK from human plasma as a growth-modulating factor.
- **Pickart L, Vasquez-Soltero JM, Margolina A. 2015.** *Oxid Med Cell Longev* 2015:648108. Gene-expression signature of GHK; copper-chelation mechanism.
- **Pickart L, Margolina A. 2018.** *Int J Mol Sci* 19(7):1987. Review of regenerative and anti-inflammatory mechanisms.

## 5. SS-31 / elamipretide (Szeto-Schiller cardiolipin-targeting tetrapeptides)

- **Zhao K, Luo G, Zhao GM, Schiller PW, Szeto HH. 2004.** *J Biol Chem* 279(33):34682. Original characterization of SS-peptide family; mitochondrial targeting without membrane potential.
- **Birk AV, Liu S, Soong Y, et al. 2013.** *J Am Soc Nephrol* 24(8):1250. Cardiolipin binding as the unifying mechanism; preservation of cristae architecture.
- **Szeto HH. 2014.** *Br J Pharmacol* 171(8):2029. Mechanistic review of cardiolipin-targeted therapeutics.
- **Chavez JD, et al. 2020.** *Commun Biol* 3:327. Cross-linking mass spec of SS-31–cardiolipin interaction; supercomplex stabilization.

## 6. Thymosin-β4 / TB-500

- **Goldstein AL, Hannappel E, Kleinman HK. 2005.** *Trends Mol Med* 11(9):421. G-actin sequestration as primary molecular activity; angiogenic and anti-apoptotic effects downstream.
- **Sosne G, et al. 2010.** *Expert Opin Biol Ther* 10(7):1029. Corneal wound-healing mechanism.
- **Crockford D. 2007.** *Ann N Y Acad Sci* 1112:1. Clinical-development overview retained for mechanistic context (not as efficacy endorsement).

## 7. Semax / Selank

**Flag — Russian-institute provenance.** Originating at the Institute of Molecular Genetics (Moscow); Western replication limited.

- **Ashmarin IP, et al. 1997.** Semax characterization (ACTH(4-7)-PGP analog). PMID:9612514.
- **Medvedev et al. 2010–2017.** Molecular-effects series on Semax; BDNF expression, neuroprotection in ischemia models.
- **Selank (TP-analog)**: Kolomin et al. 2013 *Peptides* — gene-expression profile. Flagged as single-institute body of work.

---

## 8. GHRPs and the ghrelin axis

- **Bowers CY. 1998.** *J Clin Endocrinol Metab* 83:1126. Review of GH-releasing peptide pharmacology; distinct GHRH receptor. (Bowers †; full career-summary out of scope, cited for load-bearing mechanistic synthesis.)
- **Smith RG, et al. 1997.** *Science* 276:823. Identification of the GH-secretagogue receptor (GHS-R) in pituitary and hypothalamus.
- **Kojima M, Hosoda H, Date Y, Nakazato M, Matsuo H, Kangawa K. 1999.** *Nature* 402:656. Discovery of ghrelin as the endogenous GHS-R ligand. Canon-tier primary.

## 9. CJC-1295, ipamorelin, tesamorelin (PK primaries only)

Included for mechanistic/pharmacokinetic characterization. Efficacy and clinical-use framing deliberately excluded.

- **Teichman SL, et al. 2006.** *J Clin Endocrinol Metab* 91(3):799. CJC-1295 PK: DAC technology extending half-life via albumin binding.
- **Raun K, et al. 1998.** *Eur J Endocrinol* 139:552. Original ipamorelin characterization; selective GH release without ACTH/cortisol rise.
- **Falutz J, et al. 2007.** *NEJM* 357:2359. Tesamorelin (approved GHRH analog for HIV lipodystrophy). Cited as a reference for a peptide that cleared Phase III; mechanism, not endorsement.

## 10. Cerebrolysin

Porcine-brain hydrolysate, not a single peptide. Included for canon context on "neuropeptide mixtures."

- **Windisch M. 2000.** *Mech Ageing Dev* 116:111. Review of proposed neurotrophic mechanisms.
- **Masliah E, Díez-Tejedor E. 2012.** *J Neurol Sci* 322:141. Mechanistic synthesis; BDNF/GDNF-like activity hypothesis.

## 11. Melanotan-II / α-MSH analogs

Mechanism only. Non-medical use is widespread; this dossier does not address it.

- **Dorr RT, et al. 1996.** *Life Sci* 58:1777. MT-II pharmacokinetics in humans.
- **Hadley ME. 2005.** *Ann N Y Acad Sci* 1040:351. Melanocortin-receptor pharmacology review (MC1R–MC5R specificity).

## 12. Contested / low-evidence compounds

Included for transparency, flagged for thin replication.

- **Dihexa** (Nle-Tyr-Leu-ψ-Hx; angiotensin IV analog). Benoist CC, et al. 2011 *J Pharmacol Exp Ther* 338:35. **Flag — single-group provenance** (Harding lab, WSU). HGF/c-Met pathway activation proposed.
- **Tesofensine, pinealon** — index entries only; evidence base too thin for canon inclusion beyond flagging.
- Any compound for which all positive evidence traces to a single commercial sponsor is flagged inline as **industry-funded-only** on ingestion.
