# Peptides Canon — Seed

## What this is

A mechanism-of-action bibliography for bioactive peptides of theoretical interest to the biophysics branch of the Bucket canon. The unit of inclusion is a primary paper that establishes or clarifies a **molecular or cellular mechanism** — receptor binding, signaling pathway, structural interaction, biochemical characterization, or originating isolation.

## What this is NOT

- **Not a usage guide.** No dosing, no routes of administration, no cycling, no stacking, no clinical recommendations.
- **Not a clinical-trials summary.** Phase-III efficacy and regulatory status are downstream applications; they belong, if anywhere, in an outcome canon (e.g. longevity) or in the research-landscape ledger.
- **Not a product directory.** No vendor names, no sourcing guidance, no price discovery, no compound-to-vendor mapping at any level of granularity.
- **Not career biography.** Living authors are cited for their load-bearing mechanistic contribution only.

## Relation to `research-landscape/peptide-markets/`

The commercial, regulatory, and grey-market landscape for peptides is tracked separately under `bucket-canon/research-landscape/peptide-markets/` and carries an explicit non-facilitation posture. The two dossiers are intentionally **not cross-linked at the compound level**: a reader looking for mechanism stays here; a reader looking for market structure goes there; there is no bridge. This is a discipline, not an oversight.

## Provenance flags

Several compound families in this dossier have unusual evidence structures. These are flagged inline in `primary-papers.md` and should be read as epistemic caveats, not dismissals:

- **Single-lab dominance**: BPC-157 (Sikiric/Zagreb), dihexa (Harding/WSU).
- **Russian-institute provenance**: Khavinson bioregulators, Semax, Selank, pinealon. English-language translations exist but independent Western replication is thin.
- **Industry-funded-only**: flagged where all positive evidence traces to a single commercial sponsor.

## Canon-tier bar

A paper enters this dossier if it (a) reports primary mechanistic data, (b) is cited as foundational by subsequent mechanistic work, and (c) is not purely an efficacy/outcome trial. Reviews are included only where they compress a mechanistic literature that is otherwise scattered.
