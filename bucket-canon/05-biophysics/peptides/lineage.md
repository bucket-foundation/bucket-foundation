# Peptides — Lineage / Figures Network

Network of figures whose load-bearing mechanistic contributions anchor this dossier. Living authors: single load-bearing contribution, no career biography.

## Nodes

- **Vladimir Khavinson** (St. Petersburg Institute of Bioregulation and Gerontology). Short-peptide bioregulator program; epitalon mechanism and telomerase-induction claim. Card present in `canon-figures/05-biophysics/` (pass-2 addition). Flag: Russian-institute provenance for the primary corpus.
- **Predrag Sikiric** (University of Zagreb). Originated and drove the BPC-157 research program. Flag: single-lab dominance — the majority of BPC-157 primary literature is Sikiric-group.
- **Pinchas Cohen** (USC). Senior author on the MOTS-c discovery (Lee et al. 2015). Mitochondrial-derived peptide concept.
- **Changhan David Lee** (USC). First author, Lee et al. 2015 *Cell Metab*; MOTS-c identification.
- **Loren Pickart**. Isolation of GHK (1973) and decades of copper-tripeptide mechanism work.
- **Hazel H. Szeto** (Weill Cornell). SS-peptide family; cardiolipin-targeting mechanism of mitoprotection (with Peter Schiller, Université de Montréal, co-inventor on the peptide chemistry).
- **Allan L. Goldstein** (GWU). Thymosin family — original characterization of thymosin-β4 as a G-actin sequestering peptide.
- **Cyril Y. Bowers** (†, Tulane). GHRP pharmacology; distinct GH-secretagogue pathway preceding the cloning of GHS-R.
- **Roy G. Smith**. GHS-R cloning (Smith et al. 1997).
- **Masayasu Kojima / Kenji Kangawa** (National Cardiovascular Center, Osaka). Ghrelin discovery (1999) — the endogenous ligand that closed the GHS-R loop.
- **Ivan P. Ashmarin** (†, Moscow State). Semax; Russian peptide-pharmacology school. Flag: Russian-institute provenance.

## Edges (load-bearing, mechanistic)

- Bowers → Smith → Kojima: the GHRP → GHS-R → ghrelin closure is a single three-step mechanistic arc across three independent groups and is the cleanest example of canon-tier peptide mechanism in this dossier.
- Schiller ↔ Szeto: chemistry ↔ biology pairing behind the SS-peptide / elamipretide program.
- Lee ↔ Cohen: discovery pair for MOTS-c; downstream by Merry, Kim, Cobb extends into exercise and retrograde-signaling framings.
- Khavinson corpus stands largely alone in the Western literature; replication is the live open question.
- Sikiric corpus stands largely alone for BPC-157; independent replication the live open question.
