# Melanin — Figures Network (lineage)

Canon-figure cross-references for the melanin thread. Living figures cited for mechanism contributions only; no career summaries. Cross-ref `canon-figures/05-biophysics.md` where a figure card exists.

## Semiconductor line

- **John McGinness** (with Corry, Proctor) — 1974 *Science* paper, the original amorphous-semiconductor demonstration. Seed figure of the electronic-properties lineage.
- **Paul Meredith** (living) — canonical modern reviews (2006 with Sarna); hydration-dependence characterization. Mechanism-only citation.
- **Ben Mostert** (living) — 2012 PNAS resolution of hybrid electronic/ionic conduction. Student/collaborator of Meredith. Mechanism-only citation.
- **Efthimios Kaxiras** (living) — 2006 PRL first-principles structural model. Physics anchor.

## Chemistry line

- **Giuseppe Prota** (†) — mid/late-20th-century authority on melanin chemistry; *Melanins and Melanogenesis* (1992) is the reference textbook. Italian school lineage (Naples).
- **Marco d'Ischia** (living) — successor in the Naples lineage; 2013 community standards paper. Mechanism-only citation.
- **Shosuke Ito & Kazumasa Wakamatsu** (living) — chemical characterization standards (HPLC degradation markers). Mechanism-only citation.
- **Raper (1928) and Mason (1948)** — Raper–Mason oxidation pathway, historical foundation.

## Radical / redox line

- **Tadeusz Sarna** — Polish EPR / melanin radical characterization (Krakow school). Landmark 1993 chapter with Swartz.
- **Harold Swartz** — EPR spectroscopy co-author.
- **John Simon / Yan Liu** — redox and metal-chelation work on natural Sepia eumelanin.

## Neuromelanin line

- **Luigi Zecca** — Italian (Milan) founder of the modern neuromelanin-iron line. 2004 *Prog Neurobiol* paper.
- **Fabio Zucca** — collaborator; 2017 synthesis.
- **H. Fedorow** — 2005 structural comparison across melanin classes.

## Radiosynthesis line (cross-ref `radiosynthesis/`)

- **Ekaterina Dadachova** (living) — 2007 PLoS ONE core radiosynthesis paper. Mechanism-only citation.
- **Arturo Casadevall** (living) — co-author on Dadachova line; 2017 fungal synthesis with Cordero. Mechanism-only citation.
- **Charles Turick** — 2011 electron-transfer mechanism.

## Contested line — Solís-Herrera

- **Arturo Solís-Herrera** (living, Mexico) — "human photosynthesis" water-splitting hypothesis. **Flagged `contested`.** Added to `canon-figures/` pass-2. Mechanism framing only: the claim is that eumelanin photodissociates water under physiological conditions; independent replication in mainstream biophysics has not occurred. Retained for transparency; not promoted.

## Bioelectric / Becker cross-ref

- **Robert O. Becker** (†) — cited melanin as a likely semiconductor substrate of the DC bioelectric current system in *Cross Currents* (1990) and *The Body Electric* (1985). Melanin's place in canon sits directly inside the Becker thread. See `05-biophysics/becker/` for the Becker card and primary papers; see `05-biophysics/bioelectric-lineage/` for the broader network.
