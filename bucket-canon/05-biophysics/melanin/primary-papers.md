# Melanin — Primary Bibliography (annotated)

Foundation-tier only. Mechanism, not clinical. Citations + canonical URLs + brief canon-notes. No PDFs stored; OA links listed where known.

---

## 1. Semiconductor / electronic properties (the McGinness line)

- **McGinness, Corry, Proctor 1974** — *Science* 183(4127):853–855. "Amorphous semiconductor switching in melanins."
  - DOI: `10.1126/science.183.4127.853`
  - URL: https://doi.org/10.1126/science.183.4127.853
  - Canon-note: First demonstration of amorphous-semiconductor threshold switching in a biological polymer. Opens the "melanin is an organic semiconductor" thread. **Foundation paper.**

- **Meredith & Sarna 2006** — *Pigment Cell Research* 19(6):572–594. "The physical and chemical properties of eumelanin."
  - DOI: `10.1111/j.1600-0749.2006.00345.x`
  - URL: https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1600-0749.2006.00345.x
  - Canon-note: Canonical modern review. Establishes the chemical disorder model and the hydration-dependence of electronic behavior. Entry point for anyone new to the field.

- **Mostert et al. 2012** — *PNAS* 109(23):8943–8947. "Role of semiconductivity and ion transport in the electrical conduction of melanin."
  - DOI: `10.1073/pnas.1119948109`
  - URL: https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1119948109
  - Canon-note: Resolves McGinness's semiconductor claim: conductivity is hybrid **electronic + ionic (protonic)** and rises with hydration. Mechanism-definitive.

- **d'Ischia et al. 2013** — *Pigment Cell & Melanoma Research* 26(5):616–633. "Melanins and melanogenesis: methods, standards, protocols."
  - DOI: `10.1111/pcmr.12121`
  - URL: https://doi.org/10.1111/pcmr.12121
  - Canon-note: Community standards paper. Defines what counts as eu-/pheo-/neuro-/allomelanin for experimental purposes. Reference spec.

- **Bothma et al. 2008** — *Advanced Materials* 20(18):3539–3542. "Device-quality electrically conducting melanin thin films."
  - DOI: `10.1002/adma.200703141`
  - URL: https://doi.org/10.1002/adma.200703141
  - Canon-note: Engineering proof that synthetic melanin can be fabricated as a functional thin-film conductor. Bridges biophysics → bioelectronics.

## 2. Radical / redox / antioxidant role

- **Sarna & Swartz 1993** — chapter in *Pigmented Proteins* (landmark).
  - Canon-note: Landmark EPR characterization of the stable melanin free-radical population. Baseline reference for the "melanin as radical sink" framing.

- **Liu & Simon 2003** — *Pigment Cell Research* 16(6):606–618. "Isolation and biophysical studies of natural eumelanins."
  - DOI: `10.1034/j.1600-0749.2003.00098.x`
  - URL: https://doi.org/10.1034/j.1600-0749.2003.00098.x
  - Canon-note: Redox behavior and metal chelation of natural (Sepia) eumelanin. Mechanistic anchor for the antioxidant framing.

- **Ito & Wakamatsu 2003** — *Pigment Cell Research* 16(5):523–531. "Quantitative analysis of eumelanin and pheomelanin in humans, mice, and other animals: a comparative review."
  - DOI: `10.1034/j.1600-0749.2003.00072.x`
  - URL: https://doi.org/10.1034/j.1600-0749.2003.00072.x
  - Canon-note: The chemical-characterization standard (HPLC degradation markers PTCA, TTCA, AHP, 4-AHP). Foundation for distinguishing eu- vs pheomelanin chemically.

- **Kaxiras et al. 2006** — *Physical Review Letters* 97(21):218102. "Structural model of eumelanin."
  - DOI: `10.1103/PhysRevLett.97.218102`
  - URL: https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevLett.97.218102
  - Canon-note: Ab-initio / DFT-level structural model of stacked DHI/DHICA oligomers. First-principles physics anchor for the semiconductor claim.

## 3. Neuromelanin / substantia nigra

- **Zecca et al. 2004** — *Progress in Neurobiology* 73(4):465–491. "Iron, neuromelanin and ferritin content in the substantia nigra of normal subjects at different ages."
  - DOI: `10.1016/j.pneurobio.2004.01.001`
  - URL: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.pneurobio.2004.01.001
  - Canon-note: Iron-binding and life-span accumulation of neuromelanin. Foundation for the "neuromelanin as iron sink" mechanism.

- **Fedorow et al. 2005** — *Progress in Neurobiology* 75(2):109–124. "Neuromelanin in human dopamine neurons: comparison with peripheral melanins and relevance to Parkinson's disease."
  - DOI: `10.1016/j.pneurobio.2005.02.001`
  - URL: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.pneurobio.2005.02.001
  - Canon-note: Structural and chemical comparison across melanin classes. Establishes neuromelanin as a distinct structured pigment, not degradation debris.

- **Zucca et al. 2017** — *Progress in Neurobiology* 155:96–119. "Interactions of iron, dopamine and neuromelanin pathways in brain aging and Parkinson's disease."
  - DOI: `10.1016/j.pneurobio.2015.09.012`
  - URL: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.pneurobio.2015.09.012
  - Canon-note: Modern synthesis of the NM–iron–dopamine triad. Mechanism-focused; the PD clinical framing is downstream — we cite for mechanism only.

## 4. Photoprotection / UV

- **Kollias & Baqer 1987** — *Photochemistry and Photobiology* 46(5):531–537. "Absorption mechanisms of human melanin in the visible, 400–720 nm."
  - DOI: `10.1111/j.1751-1097.1987.tb04812.x`
  - URL: https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1751-1097.1987.tb04812.x
  - Canon-note: Spectroscopic foundation for the broadband monotonic absorption of melanin. Underpins the photoprotection mechanism.

- **Meredith & Riesz 2004** — *Photochemistry and Photobiology* 79(2):211–216. "Radiative relaxation quantum yields for synthetic eumelanin."
  - DOI: `10.1562/0031-8655(2004)079<0211:RRQYFS>2.0.CO;2`
  - URL: https://doi.org/10.1562/0031-8655(2004)079%3C0211:RRQYFS%3E2.0.CO;2
  - Canon-note: Quantum yield ~10⁻⁴–10⁻³. Near-complete non-radiative dissipation of absorbed UV as heat. **The mechanistic basis of photoprotection.**

## 5. Radiosynthesis / ionizing-radiation coupling

(See also `05-biophysics/radiosynthesis/SEED.md`.)

- **Dadachova et al. 2007** — *PLoS ONE* 2(5):e457. "Ionizing radiation changes the electronic properties of melanin and enhances the growth of melanized fungi."
  - DOI: `10.1371/journal.pone.0000457`
  - URL: https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0000457
  - Canon-note: Core radiosynthesis paper. Ionizing radiation alters melanin's EPR signature; melanized fungi show enhanced growth. **Shared canon with radiosynthesis/.**

- **Turick et al. 2011** — *Bioelectrochemistry* 82(1):69–73. "The role of 4-hydroxyphenylpyruvate dioxygenase in enhancement of solid-phase electron transfer by *Shewanella oneidensis* MR-1."
  - DOI: `10.1016/j.bioelechem.2011.04.008`
  - URL: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.bioelechem.2011.04.008
  - Canon-note: Electron-transfer mechanism for melanin-mediated microbial electron shuttling. Mechanistic bridge between pigment physics and microbial energetics.

- **Cordero & Casadevall 2017** — *Fungal Biology Reviews* 31(2):99–112. "Functions of fungal melanin beyond virulence."
  - DOI: `10.1016/j.fbr.2016.12.003`
  - URL: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.fbr.2016.12.003
  - Canon-note: Synthesis review of non-virulence functions of fungal (allo)melanin — thermoregulation, radioprotection, radiosynthesis. Review-tier mechanism.

## 6. Solís-Herrera "human photosynthesis" hypothesis (CONTESTED)

> **Flag: `contested`.** Included for transparency. The underlying biophysical question — can eumelanin photolyze water under physiological conditions? — is legitimate. Broad clinical claims around the hypothesis have **not achieved independent replication in mainstream peer-reviewed biophysics venues**. Canon lists these papers so the thread can be audited, not to endorse the extrapolations. Do not cite as mechanism-tier without replication.

- **Solís-Herrera et al. 2010** — *International Journal of Medical and Medical Sciences* (and follow-up papers in open-access venues). "The human photosynthesis: the unexpected intrinsic property of melanin to split the water molecule."

## 7. Melanin structure foundations (historical)

- **Raper 1928** — work on DOPA oxidation pathway. *Biochem. J.* 22(3):790.
  - URL: https://doi.org/10.1042/bj0220790 (historical)
  - Canon-note: Identified DOPA as melanin precursor. **Historical foundation.**

- **Mason 1948** — *Journal of Biological Chemistry* 172(1):83–99. "The chemistry of melanin. III. Mechanism of the oxidation of dihydroxyphenylalanine by tyrosinase."
  - URL: https://doi.org/10.1016/S0021-9258(18)35614-X (historical)
  - Canon-note: The tyrosinase-catalyzed oxidation mechanism. Co-historical foundation with Raper ("Raper–Mason pathway").

- **Prota 1988, 1992** — *Melanins and Melanogenesis* (Academic Press, book).
  - Canon-note: The textbook synthesis of mid-20th-century melanin chemistry. Reference book, not paper — cited for completeness of the lineage.
