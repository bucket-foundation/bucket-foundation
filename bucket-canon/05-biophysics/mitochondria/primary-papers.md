# Mitochondria — Primary Papers (Annotated)

Foundation-tier only. Sub-themes ordered by foundational weight. DOIs where known; canonical URLs for pipeline ingestion live in `queries.txt`.

## 1. Endosymbiosis / evolution (5)

- **Margulis L. 1970.** *Origin of Eukaryotic Cells.* Yale University Press.
  First book-length defense of serial endosymbiosis (SET). Landscape-adjacent (book, not paper) but **foundational**: the axiomatic source for the endosymbiont reading of the mitochondrion. Canon-eligible as edition-of-record.

- **Gray MW, Burger G, Lang BF. 1999.** "Mitochondrial evolution." *Science* 283(5407):1476–1481. DOI: `10.1126/science.283.5407.1476`.
  Consolidates phylogenetic placement of mtDNA inside α-proteobacteria and the single-origin hypothesis. Canon.

- **Martin W, Müller M. 1998.** "The hydrogen hypothesis for the first eukaryote." *Nature* 392(6671):37–41. DOI: `10.1038/32096`.
  Reframes the origin event as metabolic symbiosis (H₂-producer + H₂-consumer), not predation. Canon.

- **Lane N, Martin W. 2010.** "The energetics of genome complexity." *Nature* 467(7318):929–934. DOI: `10.1038/nature09486`.
  Thermodynamic argument that bioenergetic membranes distributed across many small genomes is the enabling condition for eukaryotic complexity. **High-leverage canon entry.**

- **Roger AJ, Muñoz-Gómez SA, Kamikawa R. 2017.** "The origin and diversification of mitochondria." *Current Biology* 27(21):R1177–R1192. DOI: `10.1016/j.cub.2017.09.015`.
  Modern synthesis review. Canon.

## 2. Chemiosmosis / bioenergetics (5)

- **Mitchell P. 1961.** "Coupling of phosphorylation to electron and hydrogen transfer by a chemi-osmotic type of mechanism." *Nature* 191(4784):144–148. DOI: `10.1038/191144a0`.
  **The axiom.** Highest-weight single paper in this dossier.

- **Mitchell P. 1966.** "Chemiosmotic coupling in oxidative and photosynthetic phosphorylation." *Biological Reviews* 41(3):445–502.
  Full treatment of the hypothesis. Canon.

- **Boyer PD. 1993.** "The binding change mechanism for ATP synthase — some probabilities and possibilities." *Biochimica et Biophysica Acta* 1140(3):215–250. DOI: `10.1016/0005-2728(93)90063-L`.
  Binding-change mechanism for F₁-ATPase rotational catalysis. Nobel 1997. Canon.

- **Abrahams JP, Leslie AGW, Lutter R, Walker JE. 1994.** "Structure at 2.8 Å resolution of F₁-ATPase from bovine heart mitochondria." *Nature* 370(6491):621–628. DOI: `10.1038/370621a0`.
  First high-res F₁ structure (Walker; Nobel 1997). Canon. *(Substituted for Walker & Dickson 2006 as the foundational entry; WD 2006 is landscape-adjacent review.)*

- **Nicholls DG, Ferguson SJ. 2013.** *Bioenergetics* (5th ed.). Academic Press.
  Textbook edition-of-record. Landscape-adjacent but canon-citable for principle consolidation.

## 3. mtDNA, disease, human origins (5)

- **Cann RL, Stoneking M, Wilson AC. 1987.** "Mitochondrial DNA and human evolution." *Nature* 325(6099):31–36. DOI: `10.1038/325031a0`.
  "Mitochondrial Eve." Canon.

- **Wallace DC. 1999.** "Mitochondrial diseases in man and mouse." *Science* 283(5407):1482–1488. DOI: `10.1126/science.283.5407.1482`.
  Wallace's foundational frame. Canon.

- **Wallace DC. 2005.** "A mitochondrial paradigm of metabolic and degenerative diseases, aging, and cancer: a dawn for evolutionary medicine." *Annual Review of Genetics* 39:359–407. DOI: `10.1146/annurev.genet.39.110304.095751`.
  **The mitochondrial paradigm paper.** Canon.

- **Wallace DC. 2010.** "Mitochondrial DNA mutations in disease and aging." *Environmental and Molecular Mutagenesis* 51(5):440–450. DOI: `10.1002/em.20586`. Canon.

- **Ingman M, Kaessmann H, Pääbo S, Gyllensten U. 2000.** "Mitochondrial genome variation and the origin of modern humans." *Nature* 408(6813):708–713. DOI: `10.1038/35047064`.
  Full mtDNA-sequence revision of the Eve model. Canon.

- *(reference, not numbered)* **Lott MT, Leipzig JN, Derbeneva O, Xie HM, Chalkia D, Sarmady M, Procaccio V, Wallace DC. 2013.** "mtDNA variation and analysis using MITOMAP and MITOMASTER." *Current Protocols in Bioinformatics* 44:1.23. DOI: `10.1002/0471250953.bi0123s44`. The MITOMAP reference. Landscape-adjacent tool paper.

## 4. Cytochrome c oxidase enzymology (4)

- **Wikström M. 1977.** "Proton pump coupled to cytochrome c oxidase in mitochondria." *Nature* 266(5599):271–273. DOI: `10.1038/266271a0`.
  Foundational evidence that COX is a proton pump, not just an electron sink. Canon.

- **Tsukihara T, et al. 1996.** "The whole structure of the 13-subunit oxidized cytochrome c oxidase at 2.8 Å." *Science* 272(5265):1136–1144. DOI: `10.1126/science.272.5265.1136`.
  First full COX crystal structure. Canon.

- **Yoshikawa S, et al. 1998.** "Redox-coupled crystal structural changes in bovine heart cytochrome c oxidase." *Science* 280(5370):1723–1729. DOI: `10.1126/science.280.5370.1723`. Canon.

- **Belevich I, Verkhovsky MI, Wikström M. 2006.** "Proton-coupled electron transfer drives the proton pump of cytochrome c oxidase." *Nature* 440(7085):829–832. DOI: `10.1038/nature04619`. Canon.

## 5. Mitochondrial signaling / retrograde / ROS (4)

- **Liu Z, Butow RA. 2006.** "Mitochondrial retrograde signaling." *Annual Review of Genetics* 40:159–185. DOI: `10.1146/annurev.genet.40.110405.090613`. Canon.

- **Murphy MP. 2009.** "How mitochondria produce reactive oxygen species." *Biochemical Journal* 417(1):1–13. DOI: `10.1042/BJ20081386`.
  Site-specific mechanism of mitochondrial ROS production. Canon.

- **Sena LA, Chandel NS. 2012.** "Physiological roles of mitochondrial reactive oxygen species." *Molecular Cell* 48(2):158–167. DOI: `10.1016/j.molcel.2012.09.025`.
  ROS as signal, not damage. Canon.

- **Chandel NS. 2014.** "Mitochondria as signaling organelles." *Cell Metabolism* 20(3):394–401. DOI: `10.1016/j.cmet.2014.01.012` *(verify)*.
  The reframe review. Canon.
