# Robert O. Becker, M.D. (1923–2008)

Orthopedic surgeon and bioelectric researcher. Principal investigator at the
Veterans Administration Hospital, Syracuse, NY, and faculty at SUNY Upstate
Medical Center. Twice nominated for the Nobel Prize. Middleton Award (1964,
VA's highest research honor) for work on direct-current electrical control of
tissue growth.

## Research trajectory

Becker's program, running ~1958–1990, tested whether an analog, semiconductor-
like direct-current (DC) control system operates in parallel with the digital
nerve-impulse system and governs growth, healing, and regeneration. Principal
lines of evidence he and his collaborators reported:

- **Steady DC potentials** across the body surface that map onto the
  peripheral and central nervous system (with C. A. L. Bassett, C. Bachman,
  F. Brown).
- **Current-of-injury reversal** during limb regeneration in salamanders
  (*Ambystoma*) and partial-limb regeneration induced in rats after small
  applied currents (Becker & Spadaro).
- **Bone as a piezoelectric / semiconductor material**: stress-generated
  potentials, dielectric dispersion, photoconductivity, and EPR evidence for
  charge-carrier behavior in collagen and bone mineral.
- **Silver-ion electrochemistry** for osteomyelitis: electrically generated
  silver ions ("iontophoretic" delivery) as an antimicrobial and
  dedifferentiation-inducing agent (Becker, Spadaro, Berger).
- **Environmental electromagnetic fields**: biological effects of ELF/VLF
  fields from power lines, submarine-communication systems (Project Sanguine
  / Seafarer), and early mobile-radio infrastructure. Congressional testimony
  and the 1985 New York State Powerlines Project.

## Public-facing work

*The Body Electric* (1985, with Gary Selden) is the canonical popular
account of the research program. *Cross Currents* (1990) extends into
environmental-EMF policy. *Electromagnetism and Life* (1982, with Marino) is
the technical monograph.
