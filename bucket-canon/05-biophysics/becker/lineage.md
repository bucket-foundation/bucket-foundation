# Becker lineage — who carries the bioelectric thread

Figures whose work explicitly builds on, cites, or extends Becker's
DC-control-system / bioelectric-regeneration program. Ordered by how direct
the lineage is. Mechanism-focused, neutral tone.

## Direct collaborators

### Andrew A. Marino
Becker's closest long-term scientific partner. Coauthor of *Electromagnetism
and Life* (1982). Continued the EMF-bioeffects program at LSU Medical Center
after Becker's retirement. Primary expositor of Becker's experimental record
to the legal and regulatory world.
- Marino & Becker, "Electrical stimulation of partial limb regeneration in
  mammals" (*Nature* 1972).
- Marino, *Going Somewhere* (2010) — autobiography documenting the Becker
  years.

### Joseph A. Spadaro
Becker's bench collaborator on silver-ion and bone-growth electrochemistry.
Multiple coauthorships 1974–1995 (see `papers.bib`, entries with
Spadaro, J.).

### Maria Reichmanis
Coauthor on the DC-potential measurements at acupuncture points
(Reichmanis, Marino, Becker, *IEEE Transactions on Biomedical Engineering*,
1975).

## Second-generation / cites-as-foundational

### James L. Oschman
*Energy Medicine: The Scientific Basis* (2000, 2nd ed. 2015). Oschman treats
Becker's semiconductor-bone work and DC field maps as one of the three
foundational pillars of his framework (alongside Szent-Györgyi's
electronic-biology and Ho's coherent-fields work). Extends to the living
matrix / connective-tissue continuum.

### Mae-Wan Ho
*The Rainbow and the Worm* (1993, 3rd ed. 2008). Ho cites Becker for the DC
body-field evidence and integrates it into a coherent-quantum-state model of
the organism. Key work: Ho, "Quantum coherence and conscious experience"
(1997).

### Abraham R. Liboff
Independent theorist of ion-cyclotron-resonance ELF effects. Cites Becker
repeatedly as the empirical anchor for the claim that weak ELF fields have
specific biological targets. Key work: Liboff, "Geomagnetic cyclotron
resonance in living cells" (*Journal of Biological Physics*, 1985).

### Reba Goodman & Martin Blank
Columbia biochemists who documented ELF-induced stress-protein (HSP70) and
DNA transcription effects. Cite Becker's DC/ELF framework as the motivating
hypothesis. Key work: Blank & Goodman, "Electromagnetic fields stress living
cells" (*Pathophysiology*, 2009).

### Carl F. Blackman
NIEHS researcher. Calcium-efflux "windows" in ELF exposure (Blackman et al.,
*Bioelectromagnetics*, 1982). Cites Becker as first-line evidence that the
nervous system is electromagnetically tunable.

### Cyril W. Smith
Electrical engineer, coauthor with Herbert Fröhlich. *Electromagnetic Man*
(Smith & Best, 1989) places Becker's DC-field work inside the Fröhlich
coherent-oscillation framework.

### Gerald H. Pollack
*Cells, Gels and the Engines of Life* (2001) and *The Fourth Phase of Water*
(2013). Pollack's exclusion-zone (EZ) water thesis cites Becker's
semiconductor-bone and current-of-injury results as precedent for charge
separation inside living tissue.

### Michael Levin (Tufts, Allen Discovery Center)
Explicit acknowledgment of Becker as intellectual precursor in the modern
bioelectric-morphogenesis program. Levin's bioelectric-code / regenerative-
pattern papers revive the Becker thesis with molecular-voltage-reporter
tools. Key works: Levin, "Molecular bioelectricity: how endogenous voltage
potentials control cell behavior and instruct pattern regulation in vivo"
(*Molecular Biology of the Cell*, 2014); Levin & Martyniuk, "The bioelectric
code" (*Biosystems*, 2018). Levin has named Becker in interviews and review
essays as the figure who kept the bioelectric research program alive
through the 1970s–1980s.

## Precursors Becker himself cited

- **Elmer J. Lund** (1947, *Bioelectric Fields and Growth*) — Becker's
  principal acknowledged forerunner for the DC-field / morphogenesis link.
- **Harold Saxton Burr** (*The Fields of Life*, 1972, collecting work from
  the 1930s–50s) — Yale anatomist who mapped steady-state "L-fields" on
  organisms. Becker treated Burr as the earliest systematic empirical
  evidence.
- **Albert Szent-Györgyi** — *Bioelectronics* (1968). Becker cited him for
  the solid-state-semiconductor thesis of protein and collagen.
- **Herbert Fröhlich** — coherent-oscillation biophysics; cited in Becker &
  Marino 1982 as the theoretical scaffold for long-range EM coupling in
  tissue.
