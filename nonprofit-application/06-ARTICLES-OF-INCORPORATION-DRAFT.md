# Articles of Incorporation — DRAFT (Delaware Nonstock Nonprofit)

> **Status: DRAFT — not filed.** This document is included in the application packet as the **alternate path** if the fiscal sponsorship route is rejected or if the Foundation later decides to incorporate directly. It is written to satisfy Delaware General Corporation Law § 102 (incorporation requirements) and IRC § 501(c)(3) (organizational test for tax exemption). It has **not** been reviewed by counsel and must be reviewed by counsel before filing.

### ARTICLE III — Purposes

The Corporation is organized exclusively for **charitable, educational, and scientific purposes** within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended (the "Code"), or the corresponding section of any future federal tax code, including, but not limited to:

(a) Maintaining and disseminating an open-source protocol specification for the durable, content-addressed, machine-citeable storage of primary research artifacts, free of charge to any implementer;

(b) Operating a publicly accessible reference repository ("the reference bucket") that mirrors primary research artifacts acquired through lawful, paid micropayment channels, and making such artifacts available to the public on a non-discriminatory basis;

(c) Distributing citation revenue collected through the reference bucket to the original authors of primary research, at a default rate of not less than eighty percent (80%) of net citation receipts;

(d) Supporting independent operators of compatible repositories through technical documentation, conformance test vectors, and grants;

(e) Engaging in any and all activities that are lawful and consistent with the purposes set forth above and with Section 501(c)(3) of the Code.

### ARTICLE IV — Nonstock Status

The Corporation shall not have any capital stock and shall not be authorized to issue any shares of stock of any kind. The Corporation is organized on a nonstock basis.

### ARTICLE V — Members

The Corporation shall have no members. The rights and powers that would otherwise vest in members under the General Corporation Law of the State of Delaware shall vest in the Board of Directors.

### ARTICLE VII — Limitations on Activities

(a) **No Private Inurement.** No part of the net earnings of the Corporation shall inure to the benefit of, or be distributable to, any director, officer, or other private person, except that the Corporation shall be authorized and empowered to pay reasonable compensation for services actually rendered, and to make payments and distributions in furtherance of the purposes set forth in Article III.

(b) **No Lobbying.** No substantial part of the activities of the Corporation shall be the carrying on of propaganda, or otherwise attempting to influence legislation, except as otherwise provided by Section 501(h) of the Code.

(c) **No Political Activity.** The Corporation shall not participate in, or intervene in (including the publishing or distribution of statements), any political campaign on behalf of, or in opposition to, any candidate for public office.

(d) **Section 501(c)(3) Override.** Notwithstanding any other provision of these Articles, the Corporation shall not carry on any other activities not permitted to be carried on (i) by a corporation exempt from federal income tax under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code, or (ii) by a corporation, contributions to which are deductible under Section 170(c)(2) of the Code.

### ARTICLE VIII — Dissolution

Upon the dissolution of the Corporation, the Board of Directors shall, after paying or making provision for the payment of all of the liabilities of the Corporation, dispose of all of the assets of the Corporation exclusively for the purposes of the Corporation in such manner, or to such organization or organizations organized and operated exclusively for charitable, educational, religious, or scientific purposes as shall at the time qualify as an exempt organization or organizations under Section 501(c)(3) of the Code, as the Board of Directors shall determine. Any such assets not so disposed of shall be disposed of by the Court of Chancery of the County in which the principal office of the Corporation is then located, exclusively for such purposes or to such organization or organizations, as said Court shall determine, which are organized and operated exclusively for such purposes.

**The Corporation expresses an explicit preference, but not a binding direction**, that any residual assets upon dissolution be transferred to one or more of the following organizations (or their successors), provided each remains a § 501(c)(3) (or equivalent) entity in good standing at the time of dissolution:

- The Internet Archive
- arXiv (operated by Cornell University)
- Code for Science & Society
- The Software Freedom Conservancy
- Any successor entity to bucket.foundation that operates a compatible bucket under the same protocol

## Filing checklist (alternate path — DO NOT execute without counsel)

- [ ] Engage Delaware registered agent (~$50–$200/yr)
- [ ] File Certificate of Incorporation with Delaware Division of Corporations (filing fee ~$89 for nonstock nonprofit)
- [ ] Obtain Delaware Business License (if required for activities in DE)
- [ ] Apply for federal Employer Identification Number (EIN) via IRS Form SS-4
- [ ] Adopt Bylaws at organizational meeting of Board of Directors
- [ ] Adopt Conflict of Interest Policy (use `05-CONFLICT-OF-INTEREST-POLICY.md`)
- [ ] Open bank account in the Corporation's name (requires EIN + Articles + Bylaws + board resolution)
- [ ] File IRS Form 1023 or 1023-EZ (eligibility worksheet at `07-FORM-1023-EZ-WORKSHEET.md`)
- [ ] Register for state charitable solicitation in any state where the Corporation will solicit donations (varies by state; CA, NY, FL are most aggressive)
- [ ] Annual Delaware franchise tax / annual report ($25 minimum for nonprofits)

**Estimated one-time cost to incorporate + apply for 501(c)(3):** $400 (Form 1023-EZ path, eligible) to $2,500 (Form 1023 long path) plus optional legal review at $1,500–$5,000.

**Estimated annual ongoing cost:** $200–$500 (registered agent + Delaware franchise tax + state charitable registration renewals).

This is the primary reason the Foundation is pursuing fiscal sponsorship first: at year-one expected revenue under $2,000, the cost of direct incorporation is a meaningful fraction of operating budget, and the time to complete IRS review (3–12 months) blocks the Foundation from issuing tax receipts for that whole period.
