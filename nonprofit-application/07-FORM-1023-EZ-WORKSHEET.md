**Tentative conclusion:** The Foundation is **likely eligible** for Form 1023-EZ at the moment of filing, **provided** that:

1. **Question 1 (gross receipts).** We file in year 1 or year 2, while annual receipts are still well under $50,000. Filing in year 3 may push us over the threshold and require the long Form 1023.
2. **Question 13 (unrelated business income).** We obtain a written opinion that **citation fees collected on behalf of authors are not unrelated business income** to the Foundation. The Foundation's position is that citation fees are program-related revenue (they fund the charitable purpose directly) and that the author payouts pass through the Foundation in a fiduciary capacity. Counsel review required.
3. **Question 19 (joint ventures).** No commercial joint venture with AGFarms LLC is entered before filing.

| 10 | If denied or borderline: stay under sponsorship indefinitely | — |

**Cost of this path (assuming 1023-EZ eligibility holds):**

- Delaware filing fee: ~$89
- Registered agent (year 1): ~$150
- IRS user fee: $275
- Counsel review (UBI opinion + bylaws review): $1,000–$2,500
- **Total:** ~$1,500–$3,000

This is well under the long Form 1023 path ($600 user fee + significantly more legal time, total $4,000–$10,000) and is the reason the Foundation will work hard to stay under the $50,000 receipt threshold in years 1 and 2.

## What the Foundation will NOT do to preserve eligibility

- Refuse legitimate donations or grants in order to stay under the $50,000 threshold. If genuine philanthropic capital is offered, the Foundation will accept it and switch to the long-form path.
- Delay paying authors in order to keep cash receipts low at year-end. Author payouts are programmatic activity, not deferrable.
- Decline to enter a beneficial commercial agreement with AGFarms LLC purely to preserve eligibility, **provided** the agreement is on arm's-length terms and approved under the COI policy.

Eligibility is a convenience, not a constraint on the mission.
